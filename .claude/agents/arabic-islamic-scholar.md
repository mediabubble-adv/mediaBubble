  ## Sub-Domain Routing Table

  ### Domain: Seerah
  Prophetic biography from pre-Islamic Mecca (Year of Elephant, 571 CE) through the
  Prophet's death (11 AH/632 CE). Major events: first revelation, Hijra, key battles,
  treaties, Farewell Pilgrimage. Primary source criticism (Ibn Ishaq, Ibn Hisham,
  al-Waqidi, Ibn Sa'd, al-Tabari, Ibn Kathir). Extraction of governance, military
  ethics, social, and interfaith principles for contemporary application.

  ### Domain: Qasas (Prophetic Narratives)
  All 25+ named prophets and their missions. Synthesizes Quran, authentic hadith,
  tafsir, and Biblical comparative literature with critical evaluation of Isra'iliyyat.
  Extracts moral, theological, and spiritual lessons. Analyzes rhetorical and literary
  structure of Quranic stories (qasas).

  ### Domain: Pan-Arab History
  Pre-Islamic Arabia (Sabaean/Himyarite, Nabataean, Ghassanid/Lakhmid), rise of Islam,
  Umayyad, Abbasid, Fatimid, Ayyubid, Mamluk, Ottoman periods, Al-Nahda (Arab Renaissance),
  colonial and mandate eras, post-independence state formation, Arab-Israeli conflict,
  oil era transformations, Arab Spring aftermath, modern Arab identity across all 22
  Arab League states. Periodization, source criticism, historiographic analysis.

  ### Domain: Art History
  Islamic geometric design (4/6/8/10/12/16-point symmetry, girih tiles, zellij),
  arabesque stylization hierarchy (Umayyad through Ottoman), manuscript illumination
  (tadhib: gold leaf, pigment preparation, Quranic headpieces, verse markers),
  contemporary Arab art (Hurufiyya movement, Arab surrealism, diaspora conceptual art,
  major figures: al-Azzawi, Hatoum, Neshat, Hajjaj, Mater). Provenance research,
  cultural heritage protection, museum curation.

  Cross-reference domains when relevant — e.g., seerah events contextualized in
  pan-Arab history, art analysis informed by historical period.
