# Seerah Sources — Primary & Secondary Reference

## Primary Sources

### Biographical Corpus
| Source | Author | Death | Coverage | Reliability |
|--------|--------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Sirat Ibn Ishaq | Muhammad ibn Ishaq (d. 151 AH) | 767 CE | Birth to death | Foundation text, preserved through Ibn Hisham |
| Sirat Ibn Hisham | Abd al-Malik ibn Hisham (d. 218 AH) | 833 CE | Birth to death | Edited/abridged Ibn Ishaq, added annotations |
| Al-Maghazi | Al-Waqidi (d. 207 AH) | 823 CE | Military campaigns | Detailed but some isnad weaknesses |
| Tabaqat al-Kubra | Ibn Sa'd (d. 230 AH) | 845 CE | Biographical layers | Most comprehensive early biographical dictionary |
| Tarikh al-Tabari | Al-Tabari (d. 310 AH) | 923 CE | Universal history | Chronological annals with multiple chains |
| Al-Bidaya wa'l-Nihaya | Ibn Kathir (d. 774 AH) | 1373 CE | Creation to present | Hadith-focused, critical evaluation |

- Sahih Muslim — Kitab al-Jihad, Kitab al-Fada'il
- Sunan Abu Dawud — Kitab al-Jihad
- Jami' al-Tirmidhi — Manaqib, Tafsir
- Sunan al-Nasa'i — Kitab al-Jihad
- Sunan Ibn Majah — Kitab al-Jihad
- Muwatta Imam Malik — Kitab al-Jihad
- Musnad Ahmad ibn Hanbal — extensive Seerah narrations

### Specialized Works
- Dala'il al-Nubuwwah (al-Bayhaqi) — proofs of prophethood
- Al-Shama'il al-Muhammadiyyah (al-Tirmidhi) — prophetic characteristics
- Riyadh al-Salihin (al-Nawawi) — ethical teachings with Seerah context

## Chronological Framework

### Pre-Islamic Period (571–610 CE)
- Year of Elephant (~571 CE) — Abraha's expedition
- Birth of Muhammad in Mecca
- Death of Aminah (age 6) — care by Abd al-Muttalib
- Death of Abd al-Muttalib (age 8) — care by Abu Talib
- Journey to Syria (age 9/12) — meeting Bahira
- Participation in Hilf al-Fudul (age 20)
- Marriage to Khadijah (age 25)
- Reconstruction of Ka'bah (age 35) — placement of Black Stone

### Meccan Period (610–622 CE) — 13 Years
- First revelation in Cave Hira (age 40)
- Secret preaching (3 years)
- Public preaching on Mount Safa
- Persecution of early Muslims
- First migration to Abyssinia (615 CE)
- Second migration to Abyssinia
- Year of Sorrow (619 CE) — death of Khadijah and Abu Talib
- Journey to Ta'if
- Isra' and Mi'raj
- Pledges of Aqabah (1st and 2nd)

### Medinan Period (622–632 CE) — 10 Years
- Hijra to Medina (622 CE)
- Constitution of Medina
- Battle of Badr (2 AH / 624 CE)
- Battle of Uhud (3 AH / 625 CE)
- Battle of Khandaq/Trench (5 AH / 627 CE)
- Treaty of Hudaybiyyah (6 AH / 628 CE)
- Conquest of Khaybar (7 AH / 628 CE)
- Conquest of Mecca (8 AH / 630 CE)
- Battle of Hunayn (8 AH / 630 CE)
- Expedition to Tabuk (9 AH / 630 CE)
- Farewell Pilgrimage (10 AH / 632 CE)
- Death of the Prophet (11 AH / 632 CE)

## Critical Methodology

### Source Evaluation Criteria
1. **Isnad (chain of transmission)** — continuity, narrator reliability, absence of anomalies
2. **Matn (text) analysis** — internal consistency, Quranic alignment, historical plausibility
3. **Cross-referencing** — multiple independent chains (mutawatir vs. ahad)
4. **Temporal proximity** — closeness to events described
5. **Authorial methodology** — critical vs. uncritical compilation

### Common Points of Scholaric Disagreement
- Exact dates of early events
- Numbers in battle accounts
- Sequence of some expeditions
- Interpretation of specific prophetic actions
- Authenticity of certain narrations
