# Pan-Arab Timeline — Complete Periodization

## Pre-Islamic Arabia (~3000 BCE–622 CE)

### South Arabian Kingdoms
| Kingdom | Period | Capital | Notes |
|---------|--------|---------|-------|
| Saba (Sheba) | ~1200 BCE–275 CE | Ma'rib | Dam of Ma'rib, frankincense trade |
| Himyar | 110 BCE–525 CE | Zafar | Last pre-Islamic South Arabian kingdom |
| Awsan | 8th c. BCE–3rd c. CE | — | Rival of Saba |
| Ma'in | 6th–2nd c. BCE | Qarnaw | Incense trade network |
| Hadramawt | 8th c. BCE–3rd c. CE | Shabwa | Frankincense production |

### North Arabian Entities
| Entity | Period | Location | Notes |
|--------|--------|----------|-------|
| Nabataeans | 4th c. BCE–106 CE | Petra (Jordan) | Trade empire, Arabic inscriptions, Roman annexation |
| Ghassanids | 3rd–7th c. CE | Syria/Jordan | Byzantine client kingdom, Monophysite Christian |
| Lakhmids | 3rd–7th c. CE | Hira (Iraq) | Sassanid client kingdom, Nestorian Christian |
| Kindah | 5th–6th c. CE | Central Arabia | First attempt at Arabian unification |

### Jahiliyyah Culture
- Poetry as supreme art form (Mu'allaqat al-Sab')
- Tribal honor system (muruwwa)
- Oral tradition and genealogical knowledge
- Polytheistic religion with Ka'bah as pilgrimage center
- Annual markets (Souq 'Ukaz, Dhu al-Majaz, Majanna)

## Rise of Islam (610–661 CE)

### Prophetic Era (610–632)
- 610: First revelation
- 622: Hijra to Medina, Islamic calendar begins
- 624: Battle of Badr
- 625: Battle of Uhud
- 627: Battle of Khandaq
- 628: Treaty of Hudaybiyyah
- 630: Conquest of Mecca
- 632: Farewell Pilgrimage, death of the Prophet

### Rashidun Caliphate (632–661)
| Caliph | Reign | Key Events |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Abu Bakr | 632–634 | Ridda Wars, compilation of Quran |
| Umar ibn al-Khattab | 634–644 | Conquest of Syria, Egypt, Iraq, Persia; diwan system |
| Uthman ibn Affan | 644–656 | Standardization of Quran text, naval expansion |
| Ali ibn Abi Talib | 656–661 | First Fitna, Battle of Camel, Battle of Siffin |

## Umayyad Era (661–750 CE)

| Caliph | Reign | Key Events |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Mu'awiya I | 661–680 | Damascus capital, naval expansion, hereditary succession |
| Yazid I | 680–683 | Karbala (680), siege of Mecca |
| Abd al-Malik | 685–705 | Arabic coinage, Dome of the Rock, administrative Arabization |
| Al-Walid I | 705–715 | Umayyad Mosque, expansion to Spain and Sindh |
| Umar II | 717–720 | Reformist caliph, mawali policy |
| Hisham | 724–743 | Last strong Umayyad, Battle of Tours (732) |

**Key Features:** Arab aristocracy, Syrian military dominance, cultural synthesis with Byzantine/Persian traditions, construction of great mosques, expansion to Spain (711) and Central Asia.

## Abbasid Era (750–1258 CE)

### Early Abbasid (750–861)
| Caliph | Reign | Key Events |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Al-Saffah | 750–754 | Abbasid revolution, elimination of Umayyads |
| Al-Mansur | 754–775 | Foundation of Baghdad (762) |
| Harun al-Rashid | 786–809 | Golden Age, House of Wisdom, Thousand and One Nights setting |
| Al-Ma'mun | 813–833 | Translation movement, Mu'tazila, Bayt al-Hikma |

### Middle Abbasid (861–1055)
- Anarchy at Samarra (861–870) — Turkish military dominance
- Rise of autonomous dynasties: Tulunids, Ikhshidids, Samanids, Buyids
- Buyid control of Baghdad (945–1055) — caliph as figurehead

### Late Abbasid (1055–1258)
- Seljuk control (1055) — Nizam al-Mulk, Nizamiyya madrasas
- Crusader period (1096–1291)
- Mongol conquest and fall of Baghdad (1258)

**Golden Age Achievements:** Algebra (al-Khwarizmi), optics (Ibn al-Haytham), medicine (Ibn Sina), philosophy (al-Farabi, Ibn Rushd), chemistry (Jabir ibn Hayyan), geography (al-Idrisi)

## Fatimid Era (909–1171 CE)

- Ismaili Shia caliphate
- Foundation of Cairo (969) and Al-Azhar (970)
- Cultural flourishing: libraries, universities, art
- Competition with Abbasids and Umayyads of Cordoba (three caliphates)
- Decline under Saladin, reintegration into Sunni orbit (1171)

## Ayyubid Era (1171–1260 CE)

- Saladin (Salah al-Din) — Kurdish general, founder
- Recapture of Jerusalem (1187)
- Third Crusade (1189–1192) — Richard the Lionheart
- Madrasa system expansion
- Division among successors into regional principalities

## Mamluk Era (1250–1517 CE)

| Period | Dates | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Bahri Mamluks | 1250–1382 | Turkish origin, defeated Mongols at Ain Jalut (1260) |
| Burji Mamluks | 1382–1517 | Circassian origin, cultural patronage |

**Achievements:** Defeated Mongols and Crusaders, architectural legacy (Cairo's Islamic monuments), protection of holy cities, scholarly patronage (Ibn Taymiyyah, Ibn Khaldun)

## Ottoman Period (1517–1918 CE)

### Key Phases
| Phase | Period | Characteristics |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Early Ottoman rule | 1517–1700 | Provincial administration, relative autonomy |
| De facto independence | 1700–1800 | Local dynasties (Mamluks in Egypt, Zaydis in Yemen) |
| Tanzimat reforms | 1839–1876 | Modernization, centralization, European pressure |
| Hamidian era | 1876–1909 | Abdulhamid II, pan-Islamism, constitutional suspension |
| Young Turk era | 1908–1918 | Constitutional restoration, Arab nationalism, WWI |

### Key Events
- Napoleon's invasion of Egypt (1798–1801) — shock to Ottoman world
- Muhammad Ali of Egypt (1805–1848) — modernization, quasi-independence
- Arab cultural awakening begins
- Suez Canal opening (1869)
- British occupation of Egypt (1882)
- Arab Revolt (1916) — Sharif Hussein, T.E. Lawrence
- Sykes-Picot Agreement (1916) — secret colonial partition
- Balfour Declaration (1917) — Jewish national home in Palestine

## Al-Nahda — Arab Renaissance (19th–early 20th c.)

### Key Figures
- Rifa'a al-Tahtawi (1801–1873) — Egyptian modernizer
- Butrus al-Bustani (1819–1883) — Lebanese encyclopedist
- Ahmad Faris al-Shidyaq (1804–1887) — journalist, linguist
- Jamal al-Din al-Afghani (1838–1897) — Islamic reformer
- Muhammad 'Abduh (1849–1905) — Islamic modernism
- Qasim Amin (1863–1908) — women's rights

### Key Developments
- Printing press introduction and proliferation
- Newspaper and journal culture
- Translation movement (European works into Arabic)
- Educational reform (modern schools, universities)
- Literary revival (neo-classical poetry, prose modernization)
- Political awakening (constitutionalism, nationalism)

## Post-Independence (1946–2010)

### Key Movements
- Pan-Arabism (Nasser, Ba'ath Party)
- Arab nationalism vs. Islamism vs. monarchy
- Arab-Israeli conflict (1948, 1967, 1973, 1982, 2006)
- Oil boom and transformation (1970s)
- Lebanese Civil War (1975–1990)
- Iran-Iraq War (1980–1988)
- Gulf War (1990–1991)
- Oslo Accords (1993)
- Second Intifada (2000)

### Regime Types
- Republics: Egypt, Syria, Iraq, Algeria, Tunisia, Yemen, Sudan, Libya
- Monarchies: Saudi Arabia, Jordan, Morocco, Gulf states
- Hybrid: Lebanon (confessional democracy)
