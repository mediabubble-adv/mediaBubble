## Narrative Structure Analysis

### Common Qasas Pattern
1. **Sending** — Allah sends a prophet to a specific people
2. **Message** — Core call: worship Allah alone, abandon false gods/immorality
3. **Rejection** — People reject, mock, or threaten the prophet
4. **Signs** — Allah grants miracles as proof
5. **Persistence** — Prophet continues calling despite opposition
6. **Ultimatum** — Warning of divine punishment
7. **Outcome** — Believers saved, rejecters punished
8. **Lesson** — Quranic moral extraction

### Typology of Prophetic Missions
- **Warner (Mundhir)**: Nuh, Hud, Salih, Shu'ayb, Lut — primary role: warning of punishment
- **Lawgiver (Ulu al-'Azm)**: Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, Isa, Muhammad — brought new legislation
- **Reformer (Muslih)**: Shu'ayb, Dawud, Sulayman — social/economic reform
- **Miracle-Worker**: Musa, Isa, Salih — extraordinary signs
- **Patient Endurer**: Ayyub, Yaqub, Yunus — models of patience

## Isra'iliyyat — Critical Evaluation

### Definition
Narratives from Jewish and Christian traditions that entered Islamic tafsir and qasas literature through early converts (Ka'b al-Ahbar, Wahb ibn Munabbih) and exegetes.

### Classification
| Category | Treatment | Examples |
|----------|-----------|----------|
| Confirmed by Quran/Hadith | Accept | Some details of Musa-Pharaoh narrative |
| Contradicted by Quran/Hadith | Reject | Solomon's apostasy, David's sin with Bathsheba |
| Neither confirmed nor contradicted | Permissible to narrate with caution | Details of Adam's creation, dimensions of Noah's Ark |

### Key Sources of Isra'iliyyat
- Ka'b al-Ahbar (Yemeni Jewish convert)
- Wahb ibn Munabbih (Yemeni scholar)
- Abdullah ibn Salam (Jewish convert, Companion)
- Tafsir al-Tabari (contains many Isra'iliyyat with chains)
- Qasas al-Anbiya by al-Kisa'i, al-Tha'labi

## Cross-Narrative Thematic Connections

### Patience (Sabr)
- Ayyub: physical affliction
- Yaqub: loss of children
- Nuh: centuries of rejection
- Musa: Pharaoh's persecution
- Muhammad: Meccan opposition

### Divine Justice
- Destruction of 'Ad (Hud) — arrogance
- Destruction of Thamud (Salih) — transgression
- Destruction of Pharaoh's army (Musa) — tyranny
- Destruction of Lot's people — immorality
- Destruction of Madyan (Shu'ayb) — economic corruption
