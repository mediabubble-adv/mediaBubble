# Iraqi Arabic — Qeltu vs Gilit Detailed Comparison

## The Fundamental Isogloss

"I said" in Qeltu = **قلت (qeltu)** vs Gilit = **ﮔﻠﺖ (gilit)**

This single word difference marks a deep linguistic split reflecting the historical settlement patterns of Iraq:

| Feature | Qeltu (Sedentary) | Gilit (Nomadic/Bedouin-descended) |
|---------|------------------|----------------------------------|
| Qaf | ق → q (preserved) | ق → g (گ) |
| Kaf | ك → k (preserved) | ك → k or č in some contexts |
| 1st person singular prefix | **n-** (نكتب - niktib) | **a-** (أكتب - aktib) |
| 1st person plural prefix | **n-** (نكتبوا - niktbu) | **n-** (نكتب - niktib) |
| Demonstrative "this" | هاذ (hādh) / هذي (hadhī) | هذا (hādha) / هذي (hadhī) |

## Geographic Distribution

| Group | Major Cities | Population |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Qeltu (Tigris) | Mosul, Tikrit, Samarra, Baghdad (older Christian/Jewish), Basra (older) | ~30% of Iraqi population |
| Gilit (Euphrates) | Baghdad (majority), Karbala, Najaf, Nasiriyah, Basra (majority) | ~70% of Iraqi population |

## Christian & Jewish Baghdadi (Qeltu)

Now endangered (most speakers in diaspora). Distinctive features:

| Feature | Christian Baghdadi | Muslim Baghdadi (Gilit) |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Qaf | q (strong) | g |
| "I" | آني (ānī) | آني (ānī) — same but different intonation |
| "You (m)" | إنت (inta) | إنت (inta) |
| "You (f)" | إنتي (intī) | إنتي (intī) |
| Vocabulary | Syriac liturgical words | Standard Arabic source |
| Examples: "church" | كنيسة (knīsa) | كنيسة (knīsa), but "monastery" = دير → distinct |

## Moslawi (Mosul Qeltu)

| Feature | Mosul | Baghdad (Gilit) |
|---------|-------|----------------|
| Qaf | q | g |
| Emphatic consonants | Very strong | Moderate |
| Greeting | "الله يمسيك بالخير" distinctive | Standard |
| Intonation | Sing-song, rising-falling | Flatter |
| Vocabulary | Turkish: باج (tax), چاي (tea) | Shared |
