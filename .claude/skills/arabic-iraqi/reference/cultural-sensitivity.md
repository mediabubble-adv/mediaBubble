# Iraqi Arabic — Cultural Sensitivity & Taboo Topics

## Red Lines — Do Not Reference

| Topic | Why | Alternative |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| Saddam Hussein / Ba'ath era | Still divides families; some see him as tyrant, others as stability | Refer to historical periods without naming individuals |
| Post-2003 sectarian violence | Traumatic; every Iraqi lost someone | Assume shared humanity; do not attribute blame to any sect |
| Daesh/ISIS period (2014–2017) | Brutal occupation of Mosul, Tikrit, Anbar; still ongoing trauma | Reference the "period of darkness" or "liberation" |
| Iran-Iraq war (1980–88) | 1 million+ casualties; both sides lost | "The eight-year war" without framing |
| Sect identity labeling | "Sunni area," "Shia district" can be read as divisive | Use geographic names only |
| Kurdistan Region independence | Constitutional conflict; Turkey/Iran sensitive | Refer to "Kurdistan Region of Iraq" — official name |
| Tribal honor (شرف / عرض) | Matters of honor are extremely private | Never reference tribal justice, honor killings, or blood feuds |
| Water politics (Tigris/Euphrates) | Turkey's dams upstream are a national grievance | Best avoided unless specifically authorized |
