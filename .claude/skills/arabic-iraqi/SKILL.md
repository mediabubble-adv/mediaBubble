# arabic-iraqi — Iraqi Arabic (Mesopotamian)

## Overview

Iraqi Arabic (اللهجة العراقية / عراقي) represents the Mesopotamian branch of Arabic, divided by the fundamental qeltu/gilit isogloss that runs along the Euphrates:

- **Qeltu** (قِلْتُ — "I said"): spoken by sedentary populations in the Tigris region, Baghdad's older Christian and Jewish communities, Mosul, and religious minorities
- **Gilit** (گِلِتْ — "I said"): spoken by the nomadic/Bedouin-descended populations of the Euphrates region and southern Iraq, now dominant in Baghdad due to 20th-century rural migration

This is the single most important structural distinction to understand about Iraqi Arabic.

## The Qeltu vs Gilit Distinction

| Feature | Qeltu | Gilit | Example |
|---------|-------|-------|---------|
| Qaf | ق preserved (q) | ق → g (گ) | قال → qāl vs gāl |
| Kaf | ك preserved (k) | ك → č (in some) | كلب → kalib vs čalib |
| Verbal prefix | n- for 1st sg / n- for 1st pl | a- for 1st sg / n- for 1st pl | نكتب vs أكتب |
| Demonstrative (this) | هاذا / هذي | هذا / هذي (haḏa / haḏi) | |
| Negation | mā + -š | mā + -š (wider usage) | |

### Qeltu Sub-Dialects

- **Mosul** (Northern): retains more Aramaic features, ق → q strongly, unique greeting patterns
- **Baghdadi (Christian/Jewish)**: now endangered with diaspora; highly conservative phonology; preserved MSA-like vowels
- **Anatolian** (Turkey/Iraq border): isolated qeltu variants; Turkish adstrate high

### Gilit Sub-Dialects

- **Baghdadi (Muslim, modern)**: the de facto standard Iraqi dialect for media
- **Euphrates** (Anbar, rural central): closer to Bedouin roots; long-vowel preservation
- **Basrawi / Southern**: shares features with Gulf dialects; influenced by Indian Ocean trade (Swahili, Hindi, Portuguese loanwords)
- **Marsh Arab (Ahwar)**: isolated gilit variant with distinctive intonation and unique kinship vocabulary

## Core Grammar

### Verb System

| Tense | Pattern | Example |
|-------|---------|---------|
| Past | Perfective | كتب (kitab) — he wrote |
| Present | With d-/da- prefix | دأكتب (daktib) — I write |
| Future | Rāḥ- + imperfect | راح أكتب (rāḥ aktib) |
| Progressive | Dā- + imperfect | دا يكْتُب (dā yiktib) — he is writing |

### Conditional Structures

Iraqi has distinctive conditional particles:

| Particle | Meaning | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| إذا / إدا (idha / ida) | If (real) | إذا جيت — if you come |
| لو (law) | If (unreal) | لو تجي — if you were to come |
| يكو (yikū) | Might be / perhaps | يكو جاي — he might be coming |

### Negation

- Past: **mā** + verb + **-š**: مَا كَتَبَش (mā kitabaš)
- Present: **mā** + d-verb + **-š**: مَا دأكْتُبْش (mā daktibš)
- Non-verbal: **mū** / **mūb**: مو زين / موب زين (mū zēn)

### Question Words

| English | Iraqi (Gilit) | Iraqi (Qeltu) |
|---------|---------------|---------------|
| What? | شِنُو (shinu) / شگد (shgid) | شنو (shinū) |
| Why? | ليش (lēsh) | ليش (lēš) |
| How? | شْلُون (shlōn) | كيف (kēf) |
| Where? | وین (wēn) | وين (wēn) |
| When? | يِمْتَة (yimta) | متى (matā) |
| Who? | مَنْ / مَنُو (man / manu) | من (man) |

## Cultural Context

### Historical Layers in Vocabulary

| Source | Examples | Domain |
|--------|----------|--------|
| Aramaic (oldest) | تِمّ (timm — mouth), خي (khay — living) | Body, home, agriculture |
| Turkish | باج (bāj — tax), چاي (chāy — tea), چكمچ (chakmach — boot) | Administration, food, military |
| Persian | دنج (dinj — slow), كشك (kishk — dried yogurt) | Food, emotion |
| Kurdish | گوڕه (gura — big, in Kurdish areas) | Daily life |
| English (oil era) | كومبي (komby — company), پايپ (pāyp — pipe), بلوز (blōz — overalls) | Oil industry, technology |

### Sectarian & Social Language Markers

- Christian Baghdadi Arabic: distinct from Muslim Baghdadi; keeps Christian Aramaic liturgical vocabulary in daily speech; qaf preserved as q
- Jewish Baghdadi Arabic: largely extinct in Iraq (preserved in Israel); distinctive pronouns (آني āni for "I" vs آنا āna in Muslim Baghdadi)
- Shia vs Sunni: some lexical differences (e.g., names for prayer times, distinct honorifics)
- Marsh Arabs: unique vocabulary for watercraft, reeds, marsh rice cultivation

### Taboo Topics

- Ba'ath era, Saddam Hussein, post-2003 sectarian conflict — extremely sensitive
- Sectarian identity (Sunni/Shia/Kurd) — do not label or assume
- The Iran-Iraq war (1980–88) — still raw for all families
- The US invasion and occupation (2003–2011) — still deeply divisive
- The Islamic State / Daesh period (2014–2017) — traumatic for all Iraqis
- Kurdistan Region — do not conflate with federal Iraq in sensitive contexts
- Tribal justice and honor (sharaf / 'ird) — avoid references to tribal violence
