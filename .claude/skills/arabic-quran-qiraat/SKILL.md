# arabic-quran-qiraat — The Ten Canonical Readings (Qira'at 'Ashr)

## Overview

The Qira'at (قراءات, "readings") are the ten accepted canonical methods of reciting the Quran. They are NOT different versions of the Quran — they are permissible phonetic and orthographic variants transmitted through rigorously authenticated chains (sanad) from the Prophet Muhammad. Every variation has linguistic, orthographic (rasm 'Uthmani), and textual basis.

**Principle:** All ten Qira'at are equally divine revelation. Recitation in any of the ten is valid for prayer. The differences are in pronunciation, word form, and occasionally grammatical parsing — never in fundamental meaning or legal contradiction.

## Types of Qira'at Variation

### 1. Phonetic Variation (لا تغير المعنى)

Changes in pronunciation that do NOT affect meaning:

| Example | Hafs | Warsh |
|---------|------|-------|
| مَلِك (Malik) | "Maliki yawm al-din" | "Maliki yawmi l-din" (imala of alif) |
| إبراهيم | Ibrahīm with hamza under alif | Ibrahīm without hamza (Ibrāhīm) |
| Tashil (softening) | Full hamza | Hamza softened between two vowels |

### 2. Word Form Variation (اختلاف صيغ الكلمات)

Different morphological forms of the same root:

| Ayah | Hafs | Warsh | Meaning Impact |
|------|------|-------|---------------|
| 1:4 | مَالِك (owner) | مَلِك (king) | Both attributes of God — complementary |
| 2:125 | مُتَّخِذ (taking) | مُتَّخِذًا (taking as) | Grammatical parsing different but meaning same |
| 2:132 | يَعْمَلُونَ (they do) | تَعْمَلُونَ (you do) | Person shift (3rd → 2nd) |

### 3. Grammatical Variation (اختلاف نحوي)

Different parsing of the same orthographic form:

| Ayah | Hafs | Warsh | Grammatical difference |
|------|------|-------|----------------------|
| 2:124 | كَلِمَات (kalimāti, acc.) | كَلِمَات (kalimātin, gen.) | Case ending difference |
| Various | يَفْعَلُونَ (indicative) | يَفْعَلُوا (subjunctive) | Mood change |

### 4. Addition/Omission of Letters

| Ayah | Hafs | Warsh |
|------|------|-------|
| 2:9 | يُخَادِعُونَ | يُخَادِعُونَ (same, but some with يَخْدَعُونَ) |
| 20:12 | بِالْوَادِ | بِالْوَادِي (with yaa added) |

### 5. Difference in Consonantal Form (Rasm variation)

Some variations result from the early orthographic (rasm) system where:
- Certain letters could be read differently because dots were absent in early manuscripts
- Different Companions received slightly different wording from the Prophet

## Usul (Principles) of Each Qira'a

Each Qira'a has a consistent set of foundational principles (usul) applied across the entire Quran:

| Principle | Hafs | Warsh | Others |
|-----------|------|-------|--------|
| Hamza (naql) | Full hamza | Naql (move hamza to preceding vowel) | Various |
| Madd | 2 for asli, 4-5 for munfasil | 6 for munfasil | 2-6 depending |
| Imala (e/a raising) | None | Limited to specific words | Heavy in Hamza, Al-Kisa'i |
| Idgham Kabir | Limited | Widespread | Abu 'Amr has the most |
| Tashil | None | Between hamzas | |

## Relation to Fiqh (Jurisprudence)

Qira'at variations sometimes have legal implications:

| Topic | One Qira'a | Another Qira'a | Implication |
|-------|-----------|----------------|-------------|
| Wudu' (5:6) | "فاغسلوا" (wash) | Different parsing | Same action |
| Inheritance | Different readings of verse 4:12 | Minor grammatical variations that don't change rulings | Same |
| Prayer times | None affect fiqh directly | | |

All four Sunni madhahib accept all ten Qira'at as valid for prayer recitation.

## Preservation & Study

- **Ijazah** (license): Each reading is transmitted through a personal chain. A Qari' who learns a Qira'a receives an ijazah from their teacher, with their chain going back to the Prophet.
- **Shawādhdh** (irregular readings): Readings not meeting the mutawatir criterion but with some authenticity. May be used for tafsir/shariah evidence but NOT for prayer.
- **Books**: The primary source for the ten is *Tayyibat al-Nashr* by Ibn al-Jazari (833 AH). Modern editions include *Al-Muqaddimah al-Jazariyyah*.
