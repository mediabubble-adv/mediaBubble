# arabic-tafsir — Quranic Exegesis (Tafsir)

## Overview

Tafsir (تفسير, lit. "explanation" or "interpretation") is the science of elucidating the meanings of the Quran. It is the most integrative Islamic discipline, requiring mastery of Arabic linguistics (Nahw, Sarf, Balaghah), Hadith sciences, Usool al-Fiqh (legal maxims), Aqidah (theology), and historical context. A Mufassir (exegete) must synthesize all these fields to produce accurate interpretation.

## The Two Methodologies

### Tafsir bil-Ma'thur (التفسير بالمأثور)

Interpretation based on transmitted sources, in order of priority:

1. **Quran by Quran**: The most authoritative. Quranic verses explain other verses (e.g., a mujmal revealed in detail elsewhere)
2. **Quran by Sunnah**: Authentic hadith that explain, specify, or contextualize Quranic verses
3. **Companion statements (Aqwāl al-Ṣaḥābah)**: The Prophet's Companions, who witnessed revelation, transmitted their understanding
4. **Narrator statements (Aqwāl al-Tābiʿīn)**: The Successors' generation

**Key classical works of Tafsir bil-Ma'thur:**
- *Jami' al-Bayan* (Tafsir al-Tabari) — Ibn Jarir al-Tabari (d. 310 AH) — the mother of all tafsirs
- *Tafsir al-Quran al-Azim* — Ibn Kathir (d. 774 AH) — most widely used today
- *Ma'alim al-Tanzil* — Al-Baghawi (d. 510 AH)
- *Al-Durr al-Manthur* — Al-Suyuti (d. 911 AH)
- *Tafsir al-Qurtubi* (al-Jami' li Ahkam al-Quran) — focus on legal implications

### Tafsir bir-Ra'y (التفسير بالرأي)

Interpretation based on intellectual reasoning, provided it stays within the bounds of:
- Arabic linguistic norms (Nahw, Sarf, Balaghah, Wujuh wa Nazair)
- Islamic creed (Aqidah: no tashbih, no ta'til, no ta'wil without evidence)
- Established principles of Usool al-Fiqh
- Knowledge of asbab al-nuzul and nasikh/mansukh

**Key classical works of Tafsir bir-Ra'y:**
- *Al-Kashshaf* — Al-Zamakhshari (d. 538 AH) — Mu'tazili theological lens; unmatched linguistic analysis
- *Mafatih al-Ghayb* (Tafsir al-Kabir) — Fakhr al-Din al-Razi (d. 606 AH) — encyclopedic; philosophical and scientific
- *Anwar al-Tanzil wa Asrar al-Ta'wil* — Al-Baydawi (d. 685 AH) — distills Zamakhshari's linguistic analysis
- *Ruh al-Ma'ani* — Al-Alusi (d. 1270 AH) — major Ottoman-era tafsir

## Asbab al-Nuzul (Occasions of Revelation)

Every ayah that has a known circumstance of revelation must be understood in that context:

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Sabab (سبب) | The specific event, question, or situation that prompted revelation |
| Authenticity | Must evaluate sanad of the sabab report (sahih/hasan/da'if) |
| Applicability | Whether the ruling is specific to the sabab (khusus al-sabab) or general to the wording (umum al-lafz) |

**Example:** Surah 4:12 (inheritance verses) were revealed in response to specific inheritance disputes. The sabab explains the but the ruling is general.

## Makki vs Madani Classification

| Feature | Makki (Meccan) | Madani (Medinan) |
|---------|----------------|------------------|
| Period | 610–622 CE (13 years) | 622–632 CE (10 years) |
| Focus | Tawheed, prophethood, resurrection, akhlaq | Legislation, social justice, jihad, interfaith |
| Length | Short, powerful, rhymed (saj') | Long, detailed, legal |
| Address | "يا أيها الناس" (O people) | "يا أيها الذين آمنوا" (O believers) |
| Surahs | 86 (majority by number, minority by length) | 28 |

## Nasikh wa Mansukh (Abrogation)

| Type | Definition | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| Hukm + Tilawah | Ruling and text both abrogated | Rare; e.g., the "stoning verse" |
| Hukm only | Text remains, ruling abrogated | 2:180 (wills) → 4:11 (inheritance) |
| Tilawah only | Text abrogated, ruling remains | Some narrations about similar verses |

**Important:** The vast majority of the Quran is neither nasikh nor mansukh (not abrogated). Over-exaggeration of abrogation (especially in early Orientalist scholarship) is a known error.

## Muhkam vs Mutashabih

| Category | Definition | Example |
|----------|------------|---------|
| Muhkam (clear/muhkam) | Verses whose meaning is unambiguous | 112:4 (ولم يكن له كفوا أحد) |
| Mutashabih (ambiguous) | Verses whose meaning requires careful interpretation | Allegorical verses (متشابهات); attributes of God (3:7) |

The concept of **ta'wil** (interpretive explanation) applies primarily to mutashabih verses — especially the divine attributes (sifat Allah), avoiding both:
- **Tashbih** (anthropomorphism) — attributing human form to God
- **Ta'til** (denial) — denying God's attributes entirely

## Contemporary Tafsir

Modern mufassirun have addressed:
- Scientific interpretation (tafsir 'ilmi): Quran and modern science
- Social/humanist interpretation: Quran and social justice
- Feminist interpretation: Quran and gender equity
- Reformist/maqsadi interpretation: Quran and public interest (maqasid)

**Key modern works:**
- *Fi Zilal al-Quran* — Sayyid Qutb (d. 1966) — revolutionary; focus on social justice
- *Al-Tahrir wa al-Tanwir* — Muhammad Tahir Ibn Ashur (d. 1973) — maqasid-oriented
- *Al-Muntakhab* — Al-Azhar Academy (contemporary)
- *Al-Tafsir al-Wasit* — Wahbah al-Zuhayli (d. 2015)
- *Tafsir al-Sha'rawi* — Muhammad Mutawalli al-Sha'rawi (d. 1998)
