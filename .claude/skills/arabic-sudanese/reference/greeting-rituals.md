# Sudanese Arabic — Extended Greeting Protocol

## The Sudanese Greeting Sequence

In Sudanese culture, the greeting is a **social ritual**, not a functional exchange. Rushing it is disrespectful.

### Stage 1: Opening (2–5 minutes)

| Speaker | Line | Response |
|---------|------|----------|
| A | السلام عليكم (al-salām 3alaykum) | وعليكم السلام ورحمة الله وبركاته |
| A | كيفك؟ / كَيْف حَالَك؟ | الحمد لله، تمام؟ تمام الحمد لله |
| A | شنو لونك؟ (shinu lōnak — "what's your colour?") | لوني أبيض (lōnī abyaḍ — "my colour is white" = I'm fine) |

### Stage 2: State Inquiry (2–5 minutes)

| Expression | Meaning | Response |
|------------|---------|----------|
| أخبارك؟ / أخبار شنو؟ | How are you? / What's the news? | أخبار طيبة (akhbār ṭayyiba) |
| تمام؟ | You good? | تمام الحمد لله |
| عسل؟ | Honey? (= Doing well?) | كلو عسل (kullu 3asal) |
| ممكن؟ | Permissible? (= Fine?) | ممكن الحمد لله |

### Stage 3: Family Chain (3–5 minutes)

| Line | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| أهلَك تمام؟ | Your family good? |
| الأولاد تمام؟ | The children good? |
| البيت تمام؟ | The house good? |
| الجماعة تمام؟ | The group/neighbourhood good? |

**Extremely rude to skip this stage** — implies you don't care about their family.

### Stage 4: Farewell Transition

| Line | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| مع السلامة (maʿ al-salāma) | Go with peace |
| في أمان الله (fī amān illāh) | In God's protection |
| نتلاقى بكرة إن شاء الله | See you tomorrow |
