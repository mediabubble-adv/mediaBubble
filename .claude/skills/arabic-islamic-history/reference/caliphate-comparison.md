# Arabic Islamic History — Caliphate Comparison

| Caliphate | Duration | Capital | Extent | Language | Legacy |
|-----------|----------|---------|--------|----------|--------|
| Rashidun | 632–661 (29 yrs) | Medina | Arabia, Levant, Egypt, Persia | Arabic | Quran compilation, expansion foundations |
| Umayyad | 661–750 (89 yrs) | Damascus | Spain to Central Asia | Arabic (admin), Greek/Persian (local) | Arabization of administration; monumental architecture (Dome of Rock, Umayyad Mosque) |
| Abbasid | 750–1258 (508 yrs) | Baghdad, later Samarra, Cairo | Iraq to North Africa | Arabic, Persian | Golden Age; science, translation, literature |
| Fatimid | 909–1171 (262 yrs) | Mahdia → Cairo | North Africa, Levant, Sicily | Arabic, Berber | Ismaili Shia; Al-Azhar; trade prosperity |
| Ottoman | 1517–1922 (405 yrs) over Arabs | Istanbul | All Arab lands except Morocco/parts of Arabia | Ottoman Turkish (elite), Arabic (religious) | Last caliphate; codification of Islamic law (Mecelle) |

## Major Sects & Schools

| Branch | Approx % of Muslims | Key Centers | Distinctive |
|--------|---------------------|-------------|-------------|
| Sunni | ~85–90% | All | Follows four madhahib (Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, Hanbali) |
| Shia (Twelver) | ~10–15% | Iran, Iraq, Lebanon, Bahrain | Imamate of 12 Imams; Husayn's martyrdom central |
| Ibadi | ~1% | Oman, Zanzibar, Algeria | Moderate Kharijite descendant; distinct jurisprudence |
| Sufi | Cross-sect | Egypt, Morocco, Sudan, Levant | Mystical orders (tariqa); dhikr; saint veneration |
