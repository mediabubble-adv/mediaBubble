# Cultural Red Lines — Egypt-Specific Taboo Topics & Sensitivities

## Absolute Taboos (NEVER reference, even positively)

### Politics
- **No mention of the president**, his family, or the presidential institution
- **No mention of the military** or security apparatus
- **No political criticism** — even veiled, even "harmless"
- **No protests, revolutions, or political unrest** references
- **No opposition figures** or political movements
- **No mention of specific ministries** or government bodies in a critical light
- **No "before 2011" / "after 2011"** framing

### Religion
- **No religious jokes** of any kind — even "light" ones
- **No sectarian references** (Christian/Muslim comparisons or mentions)
- **No comparative religion** content
- **No religious figures** in humorous or informal contexts
- **No Quranic or Biblical quotes** outside explicitly religious content
- **No "God" (الله) in marketing hype** — "ربنا" is fine in blessings, but "God's gift" type phrasing is offensive

### Social
- **No sex or sexual innuendo** — even subtle/pun-based
- **No profanity** — Egyptian swears are severe taboos; no exceptions
- **No body part references** outside medical contexts
- **No alcohol or drug references** — even in humor
- **No gambling references** — even "bet" as a metaphor
- **No suicide or self-harm references** — even metaphorical

## Sensitive Topics (Require Careful Handling)

### Gender
- **No gender stereotypes** — jokes about women driving, men cooking, etc.
- **No appearance-based comments** — weight, height, looks
- **Address women respectfully** — use "ست الفاضل" / "أستاذة" / "حضرتك"
- **Equal treatment:** Don't market "for men" and "for women" as different quality tiers

### Class & Economics
- **No mocking poverty** or lack of education
- **No "luxury vs ordinary" framing** that shames non-luxury buyers
- **No references to "beggars" or "street children"** — even as analogy
- **No "this isn't expensive"** — don't dismiss others' financial constraints

### Regional
- **No Sa'idi (Upper Egypt) jokes** — regional humor is offensive
- **No Alexandria vs Cairo rivalry** as humor
- **No Delta vs Upper Egypt comparisons**
- **No "اهلاوي / زمالكوي" (Al-Ahly/Zamalek)** rivalries in brand content

### Generational
- **No mocking older generations' tech literacy** — "elderly can't use apps" humor
- **No "kids these days"** generational complaints
- **Respect elders** — always use formal titles when age is evident

## Humor Boundaries (What's Safe)

### Safe Topics ✅
| Topic | Example | Why It Works |
|-------|---------|--------------|
| Work struggles | "لما المارجرين يخلص في نص الشهر 😂" | Universal, self-deprecating, harmless |
| Cairo traffic | "٣ ساعات في الطريق عشان اجتماع ١٥ دقيقة" | Shared pain, nobody is offended |
| Morning meetings | "أجمل حاجة في الصبح — غير الاجتماعات ٨ الصبح" | Relatable, mild |
| Tech glitches | "الواي فاي يقطع في نص الاجتماع المصيري" | Universal, neutral |
| Customer requests | "العميل يقولك: عاوز كده. وإنت: شكراً على التوضيح 😂" | Insider industry humor |
| Office life | "الميكرويف مش بتاع تسخين — ده بتاع تذويب الثلج من كتر البرد" | Relatable across ages |

### Unsafe Topics ❌
| Topic | Why |
|-------|-----|
| Any political reference | Not worth the risk — even positive references can be misinterpreted |
| Religious jokes | Deep offense. No exceptions. |
| Appearance/body jokes | Personal, disrespectful |
| "Dumb person" jokes | Insulting in Egyptian culture |
| Sexist humor | Damaging to brand, offensive |
| Death/tragedy humor | Inappropriate in all contexts |
| Poverty/class jokes | Shames readers, damages trust |

## Religious Expressions (Safe & Expected)

These expressions are used by Egyptians of all religiosity levels in business context:

| Expression | Meaning | When to Use |
|------------|---------|-------------|
| إن شاء الله | God willing | Future plans, promises — DO use naturally |
| ما شاء الله | God has willed | Praise, admiration — DO use for compliments |
| الحمد لله | Praise be to God | Response to "how are you" — DO use |
| ربنا يخليك | God keep you | Response to thanks — DO use (very polite) |
| ربنا معاك | God be with you | Parting, encouragement — DO use |
| السلام عليكم | Peace be upon you | Formal greeting — DO use |
| بسم الله | In the name of God | Starting something — DO use when appropriate |

## Regional Content Sensitivity

| Region | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Cairo / Alexandria | Standard Masri. Avoid pretentious "I'm from here" posturing. |
| Upper Egypt (Sa'idi) | Don't use Sa'idi dialect in brand content unless specifically targeting. Cairo Masri is understood everywhere. |
| Gulf readers | If content reaches Gulf audience, reduce uniquely Egyptian expressions. Use MSA-like vocabulary with Masri-friendly structure. |
| Pan-Arab | Stay in L3–L4. Avoid L1-L2. avoid uniquely Egyptian words like "دُخان" or "كشري." |

## Time & Event Sensitivity

| Period | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Ramadan | Avoid food/drink imagery during fasting hours. Focus on family, generosity, spiritual themes. Evening content can reference iftar. |
| Eid | Early-morning content is inappropriate (everyone is at prayer or with family). Afternoon/evening is best. |
| National holidays | Positive, patriotic themes are fine. Avoid political commentary. |
| Post-tragedy | If a national tragedy occurs (accident, attack), pause all promotional content for 24-48 hours. Shift to empathetic/message of solidarity or pause entirely. |
| Friday | Friday morning/afternoon is prayer time. Low engagement expected. |
| Summer | Tourism peak for Red Sea/Hurghada. Content should reflect seasonal energy. |
