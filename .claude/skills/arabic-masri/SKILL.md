# Arabic Masri (Egyptian Arabic) Writing Skill

## Overview

**Masri** (مصري) is the spoken Arabic dialect of Egypt — the everyday language of ~110 million Egyptians. It differs substantially from Modern Standard Arabic (MSA / فصحى) in grammar, vocabulary, and pronunciation. Egyptian media (film, TV, music) has made Masri the most widely understood dialect across the Arab world.

**Why Masri matters:** Content written in Masri gets 2-3x higher engagement from Egyptian audiences than MSA content. Egyptians consume daily life, humor, entertainment, and marketing in Masri — MSA reads as distant, formal, or "news-like."

**Core principle:** Masri is not "incorrect Arabic." It is a parallel register with its own consistent grammar. Write it confidently, not apologetically.

---

## Formality Spectrum

Masri exists on a spectrum. Choosing the right rung is the single most important decision:

| Level | Name | When to Use | Example |
|-------|------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | **Street / Deep colloquial** (عامية قوية) | Peer chat, comedy, youth content, memes | "إنت بتستهبل ولا إيه؟" |
| 2 | **Daily Masri** (عامية يومية) | Social media, casual content, storytelling | "إحنا النهارده هنكلمكم عن حاجة مهمة" |
| 3 | **Polite Masri** (مصري مهذب) | Brand content, customer-facing, ads, any client-facing text | "احنا هنا عشان نساعدك توصل لأهدافك" |
| 4 | **Educated / Formal Masri** (مصري مُفصَح) | Articles, thought leadership, professional content | "السوق المصري شهد تطور ملحوظ في السنوات الأخيرة" |
| 5 | **MSA** (فصحى) | Legal, official announcements, religious text, academic papers | "تشهد السوق المصرية تطوراً ملحوظاً في الآونة الأخيرة" |

**Rule of thumb for content creation:** Stay in Level 3 (Polite Masri) for brand/marketing content unless the brief specifically asks for casual (Level 2). Never use Level 1 for anything client-facing.

## Core Grammar Patterns

### Negation

| Pattern | Rule | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| Verb negation | ما + verb + ش | بعمل → **مابعملش** (I don't do) |
| Non-verb negation | مش + noun/adjective/particle | أنا **مش** عاوز (I don't want) |
| Past tense negation | ما + past verb + ش | عمل → **ماعملش** (he didn't do) |

### Future Tense

Prefix **حـ** (ha-) instead of MSA سـ (sa-):

| Person | Masri | MSA |
|--------|-------|-----|
| I will | **حأعمل** (ha3mal) | سأفعل |
| He will | **حيعمل** (hay3mal) | سيفعل |
| She will | **حتعمل** (hat3mal) | ستفعل |

### Demonstratives

| Item | Masri | MSA |
|------|-------|-----|
| This (m) | **دا** (da) | هذا |
| This (f) | **دي** (di) | هذه |
| These | **دول** (dol) | هؤلاء |
| That (m) | **دوكا / ده** (dukha / dah) | ذلك |
| That (f) | **ديك / دي** (deyk / di) | تلك |

### Present Tense (Habitual)

Prefix **بـ** (bi-) indicates present/habitual:

| Person | Masri | MSA |
|--------|-------|-----|
| I do | أنا **بعمل** (ana ba3mal) | أنا أفعل |
| You do (m) | إنت **بتعمل** (inta bit3mal) | أنت تفعل |
| He does | هو **بيعمل** (howwa biye3mal) | هو يفعل |
| She does | هي **بتعمل** (heyya bit3mal) | هي تفعل |
| We do | إحنا **بنعمل** (ihna bin3mal) | نحن نفعل |

### Question Words

| English | Masri | MSA |
|---------|-------|-----|
| What? | **إيه؟** (eyh) | ماذا؟ |
| Why? | **ليه؟** (leyh) | لماذا؟ |
| Where? | **فين؟** (feen) | أين؟ |
| How? | **إزاي؟** (ezzay) | كيف؟ |
| When? | **إمتى؟** (emta) | متى؟ |
| Who? | **مين؟** (meen) | من؟ |
| How much? | **بكام؟** (bekam) | بكم؟ |
| Which? | **أي؟** (ay) | أي؟ |

### Object Pronouns (Verb Suffixes)

| Object | Suffix | Example |
|--------|--------|---------|
| Me | -ني / -لي | شافني (he saw me), قاللي (he told me) |
| You (m) | -ك | شافك (he saw you) |
| You (f) | -كِ | شافك (he saw you) |
| Him | -ه / -وه | شافه (he saw him) |
| Her | -ها | شافها (he saw her) |
| Us | -نا | شافنا (he saw us) |
| Them | -هم | شافهم (he saw them) |

### Possessive Suffixes

| Owner | Suffix | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| My | -ي / -بتاعي | كتابي / الكتاب بتاعي |
| Your (m) | -ك / بتاعك | كتابك |
| Your (f) | -ك / بتاعك | كتابك |
| His | -ه / بتاعه | كتابه |
| Her | -ها / بتاعها | كتابها |
| Our | -نا / بتاعنا | كتابنا |
| Their | -هم / بتاعهم | كتابهم |

## Pronunciation & Sound Shifts

Masri has consistent sound shifts from MSA. Knowing these helps with both writing (Arabic script) and detection (checking if text sounds Masri):

### Consonant Shifts

| MSA Letter | Masri Sound | Example (MSA → Masri) |
|-----------|-------------|----------------------|
| **ج** | **g** (as in "game") | جميل → **gamiil**, جامعة → **gama3a** |
| **ق** | **glottal stop / '** | قلم → **'alam**، قلب → **'alb** |
| **ث** | **t** or **s** | ثانوية → **tanawya**, ثلاثة → **talata**, حديث → **hadees** |
| **ذ** | **d** | ذلك → **dah**, ذاكر → **daker** |
| **ظ** | **z** but in Cairene often still distinct | ظاهر → **zaher** |
| **د** (in some words before ق) | disappears | بدقة → **be'a** (not common) |

### Regional Variation within Egypt

| Region | Distinguishing Feature | Example |
|--------|----------------------|---------|
| **Cairo / Delta** (standard Masri) | ق pronounced as glottal stop (') | قال → 'aal |
| **Alexandria** | ق often pronounced as g | قال → gaal |
| **Upper Egypt (Sa'idi)** | ق pronounced as g, ج pronounced as j (like MSA) | قال → gaal, جامعة → jaam3a |
| **Rural Delta** | ج may be pronounced as j in some words | |

**Default for content creation:** Use Cairene/Cairo Masri. It is the most widely understood and recognized as "standard Egyptian."

### Arabizi (Romanization) Conventions

When writing Masri in Latin script (common in comments, WhatsApp, informal social), use consistent mapping:

| Number/Letter | Arabic Sound | Example |
|--------------|-------------|---------|
| **2** | ء or ق (glottal stop) | mas'ala → **mas2ala** |
| **3** | ع (ayn) | 3arabi, sa3eed |
| **3'** | غ (ghayn) | 3'aly, 3'er |
| **7** | ح (haa) | 7abibi, 7aram |
| **5 / 7'** | خ (khaa) | 5ales / 7'ales |
| **6** | ط (taa) | 6ab, 6alab |
| **9** | ص (saad) | 9a7, 9adr |
| **8** | ق (qaf — less used, usually replaced by 2) | 8alb |
| **sh** | ش (sheen) | shams |
| **kh** | خ (khaa) — alternative to 5 | khalas |
| **gh** | غ (ghayn) — alternative to 3' | ghali |

## Cultural Context

### Egyptian Humor Style
- **Self-deprecation** — Egyptians love laughing at themselves
- **Wordplay** — Puns (جناس) are highly valued; playing on double meanings
- **Sarcasm** — Dry, ironic, often subtle. Delivered with a straight face
- **Exaggeration** — "ميت مرة" (100 times), "لو فضلنا ١٠٠ سنة" (if we stayed 100 years)
- **Structural** — Callback humor, reversal of expectation, teasing (إشارة)

### Politeness & Indirectness
- Requests are softened: "ممكن لو تكرمت..." (could you please...)
- Declining an offer: refuse 2-3 times (politely) before accepting
- Criticism: always indirect — "ممكن كده يكون أحسن لو..." (maybe this would be better if...)
- Elders: always address formally (using "حضرتك" / "أستاذ" / "باشا" / "مندوب")
- Generational: never mock or dismiss older generations' views in content

### Religious Expressions (used widely, regardless of personal religiosity)

| Expression | Meaning | When |
|------------|---------|------|
| **إن شاء الله** (inshallah) | God willing | Future plans, promises, hopes |
| **ما شاء الله** (mashallah) | God has willed it | Praise, admiration (wards off envy) |
| **بسم الله** (bismillah) | In the name of God | Starting something |
| **الحمد لله** (alhamdulillah) | Praise be to God | Response to "how are you", after finishing |
| **ربنا يخليك** (rabbena yekhalleek) | God keep you | Response to thanks, highly polite |
| **ربنا معاك** (rabbena ma3ak) | God be with you | Parting, encouragement |
| **السلام عليكم** (assalamu 3aleykum) | Peace be upon you | Formal greeting |
| **استغفر الله** (astaghfirullah) | I seek God's forgiveness | Expressing disapproval of something said |
| **لا حول ولا قوة إلا بالله** (la 7awla wa la 2wata illa billah) | No power but God | Surprise at difficulty, resignation |

### Taboo Topics (DO NOT Generate)
- **Politics** — Especially the president, military, security forces, or any political criticism
- **Religion** — Avoid sectarian content, religious mockery, comparative religious criticism
- **Sex** — No sexual innuendo, explicit content, or "adult humor"
- **Profanity** — Egyptian swears are extreme taboos. Do not use them.
- **Body parts** — Avoid anatomical references outside medical contexts
- **Anchoring to stereotypes** — Do not generate content that stereotypes Egyptians (lazy, dishonest, etc.)

### Generational Differences

| Feature | Older (50+) | Middle (30-50) | Youth (15-30) |
|---------|-------------|----------------|---------------|
| MSA influence | Higher | Moderate | Lower |
| English loanwords | Minimal | Moderate | Heavy |
| Formal address | Expected | Variable | Rare with peers |
| Speed of speech | Measured | Normal | Fast, clipped |
| Slang | Limited | Moderate | Heavy, rapidly changing |

**When writing for a general audience**, default to Middle register (Level 3 — polite Masri). It avoids alienating older readers while not sounding stiff to younger ones.

## Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

### 1. Forced / Overdone Slang
**Wrong:** "إحنا النهارده هنعمل ايفنت جامد فشخ للكل — Critical usage error
Better: **"عندنا النهارده إيفنت حلو أوي لكل الناس"**

**Principle:** One or two colloquial words per sentence is natural. Packing every word with the heaviest slang reads as parody.

### 2. Inconsistent Negation
**Wrong:** "ما عنديش مشكلة — actually that's correct

Better example: **"مش عاوز يروح" (not "ما عاوزش يروح")**

**Principle:** verb negation takes the ما-...-ش circumfix; non-verb negation uses مش. Don't mix them.

### 3. MSA Conjugation in Masri Context
**Wrong:** "هو يريد يروح" (MSA verb in a Masri sentence)
**Right:** "هو عاوز يروح" / "هو حابب يروح"

**Principle:** If the sentence is in Masri, conjugate all verbs per Masri patterns. Don't switch to MSA mid-sentence.

### 4. Gender Inconsistency
**Wrong:** "إنت بتقول إيه يابنت؟" (mixing masculine with feminine address)
**Right:** "إنتي بتقولي إيه يابنتي؟" (consistent feminine)

**Principle:** Once you address someone as feminine, all verbs, possessives, and adjectives must follow.

### 5. Wrong Demonstrative
**Wrong:** "هذا الكتاب دا" (mixing MSA هذا with Masri دا)
**Right:** "الكتاب دا" (Masri) or "هذا الكتاب" (MSA)

### 6. Arabizi Inconsistency
**Wrong:** Mixing 2/3/7 with q/a/kh in the same text
**Right:** Pick one convention and stick to it. If using numbers for letters, use them consistently.

### 7. English Overload (Franco-Arab Overuse)
**Wrong:** "إحنا هنـ schedule الـ meeting بكره"
Better: **"إحنا هنحدد ميعاد الاجتماع بكره"**

**Principle:** Some English loanwords in Masri are natural (تليفون, كومبيوتر, إيميل). Whole phrases in English mid-Masri ("نعمل schedule") sound unnatural and lazy unless the brand deliberately uses Franco-Arab voice.

### 8. Translating Idioms Literally
**Wrong:** "إرمي البالة من الشباك" translating "throw the towel" literally — the Egyptian equivalent is different
**Instead:** Use culturally equivalent idioms أو استخدم idioms صحيحة:
- "كسر حاجز" = broke through a barrier / ice
- "على قد كده" = that's all there is to it
- "نفسي في" = I really want / crave

## Authenticity Principles

### When to Use Masri
- Social media content (the default for Egypt-targeted posts)
- Casual articles, blog posts, email newsletters
- Ads, marketing copy, product descriptions for Egyptian consumers
- Branded content on Instagram, TikTok, Facebook
- Customer service, chat, informal communication
- Storytelling, narrative content, case studies

### When NOT to Use Masri
- Formal legal disclaimers, contracts
- Official government-adjacent communications
- Academic papers
- Content for pan-Arab audiences (use MSA or English)
- Content for Gulf audiences specifically (use MSA or they may find Masri dismissive)
- Religious texts or fatwa-like content (MSA only)
- Luxury ultra-premium brand positioning (unless the brand specifically uses Masri voice)

### The "Does This Sound Natural?" Check
Before publishing Masri content, check:
1. Read it aloud. Does it flow like something you'd hear in Cairo?
2. Does every verb follow Masri conjugation?
3. Is the register consistent throughout? (No sudden jump from polite to street slang)
4. Would an Egyptian actually say this naturally, or is it translated English?
5. If English loanwords exist, are they well-digested into Arabic? (تليفون ✓ vs "بسأل question" ✗)

## Transliterations for Brand & Product Names

When writing brand names, product names, or English terms in Masri content:

| Rule | Example |
|------|---------|
| Keep English brand names in English script | "MediaBubble" not "ميديا بابل" (unless brand has an Arabic identity) |
| Transliterate product terms naturally | website → ويبسايت, dashboard → داشبورد |
| Common tech terms stay as loanwords | إيميل, إنترنت, لينك, سيرفر |
| Name of months/numbers: use English or Arabic | يناير or جانفي — prefer English months (Eastern Arabic: يناير فبراير) |
| Currency: use symbol + EG | ١٠٠ جنيه or 100 EGP |

### Marketing-Specific Mistakes

- **Translating English puns literally into Masri** — They never work. Write new puns in Masri from scratch.
- **Over-hashtagging** — 3-5 relevant hashtags is max. 10+ generic hashtags looks desperate.
- **Wrong platform level** — A TikTok-write style on LinkedIn discredits the brand. LinkedIn Masri needs proof, data, professionalism.
- **Arabic SEO stuffing** — Natural Masri > keyword-stuffed MSA. Google handles Masri well now.
- **Mixing gender in product ads** — Pick: masc address for general, fem for female-targeted only. Don't mix.

### RTL-Aware Phrasing Rules

Arabic text is 25-35% longer than English. This affects UI layout:

- Shorten where possible: "أضف إلى السلة" → "أضف" (on buttons)
- Icons replace text direction: ← icon means "رجوع" not "→ رجوع"
- Ellipsis on overflow: "اقرأ المزيد..." / "عرض الكل..."
- Never clip text mid-word
- Place action icons on the **left** of Masri buttons (matching RTL reading flow)

### The 5 Pillars of Egyptian Humor

| Pillar | How It Works | Example |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| **Irony** | Saying the opposite of what's true, with a straight face | "أجمل حاجة في الزحمة إن الواحد ياخد راحته" (traffic jam) |
| **Self-deprecation** | Laughing at oneself first | "أنا اللي فاكر إن الشغل هيخلص النهارده 😂" |
| **Exaggeration** | Blowing things to absurd proportions | "لو قعدت اشتغل ١٠٠ سنة برضه مش هخلص" |
| **Wordplay** | Puns, double meanings, unexpected connections | "الفلوس مش كل حاجة — فيه كمان الكريدت كارد" |
| **Cultural callback** | Referencing shared Egyptian experience every Egyptian instantly recognizes | "لما المدير يقولك اجتماع ٥ دقايق" |

> مرة واحد دخل على شركة تسويق وقالهم: عاوز الناس كلها تعرفني.
> قالوله: يبقى الواحدة ب ٥٠٠٠ جنيه في اليوم.
> قالهم: إيه ده! أنا عاوزهم يعرفوني مش يتجوزوني!
>
> *Structure: Setup → absurd escalation → punchline that reframes the premise*

#### Structure 2: "الفرق بين..." (Comparison)
> الفرق بين الـ Marketer اللي بيحب شغله واللي بيشتغل عادي؟
> الأول بيفكر في الكامبين وهو في الحمام.
> التاني بيفكر في الكامبين وهو في الحمام برضه. بس بيفكر إنه عاوز ينام.
>
> *Structure: Two types → both start same → twist distinguishes them*

#### Structure 3: "في ناس..." (General observation)
> في ناس بتقول إن التسويق سهل.
> وفيه ناس تانية جربت تعمل A/B testing.
>
> *Structure: Innocent claim → dark follow-up. Perfect for brand humor.*

#### Structure 4: "لما..." (When — situation humor)
> لما العميل يقولك: "عاوز النتايج النهارده"
> وانت لسه مبدأتش التارجتنج. 😂
>
> *Structure: Universal relatable pain point → emoji punchline. Great for social media.*

#### Structure 5: "إزاي تعرف إنك..." (How to know you're...)
> إزاي تعرف إنك Marketer محترف؟
> لما تحلم بتحليل البيانات بدل ما تحلم بالبحر.
>
> *Structure: Category → unexpected (but true) identifier*

### Comedy Timing in Written Masri

| Technique | Effect | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Ellipsis pause** | Builds anticipation | "المفروض الكامبين يبدأ النهارده... المشكلة إنه لسه متكتبش 😂" |
| **"فجأة"** | Signals twist coming | "كنت ماشي في الشغل عادي، فجأة — ١٢ تسريب في ساعتين" |
| **"لقيت"** | Scenic setup for absurd discovery | "لما فتحت إعلاني لقيت ٣ آلاف كليك وولا عملية بيع واحدة" |
| **Repetition build-up** | Three beats, third is the punchline | "اليوم الأول: فكرت في الاستراتيجية. اليوم الثاني: غيرت الاستراتيجية. اليوم الثالث: غيرت المجال" |
| **False expectation** | Lead one way, snap another | "أجمل حاجة في الشغل إنك تتعلم كل يوم حاجة جديدة... زي إنك مش عارف تشوفلك دقيقة فاضية" |
| **Numbers escalation** | Fake specificity for humor | "بحاول أظبط الكامبين من ٣٤٧ يوم. النهارده أول يوم أحس إنه قرب يخلص" |
| **"أنا لما..."** | Relatable meme format | "أنا لما أفتح فيسبوك أشتغل وألاقي نفسي بقرّي بوستات بعد ساعة" |

### Brand-Appropriate Humor Topics ✅

| Topic | Why It Works | Example Bite |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| Work struggles | Universal, harmless | "أجمل شعور في الدنيا المارجرين لما يخلص؟ ولا محتوى النهارده؟" |
| Traffic in Cairo | Shared pain, nobody is offended | "٣ ساعات في الطريق عشان تعمل اجتماع ١٥ دقيقة — أي خدمة!" |
| Morning meetings | Relatable, mild | "لو في حاجة أسوأ من الفطار بكره الصبح — هي الاجتماعات ٨ الصبح" |
| Technology glitches | Universal, neutral | "الواي فاي يقطع في نص الاجتماع اللي يحدد مصير الشركة. قوانين الكون" |
| Customer requests | Insider industry humor | "العميل يقولك: 'عاوز كده'. وانت: 'شكراً على التوضيح' 😂" |
| Office life | Relatable across ages | "الميكرويف في الشغل مش بتاع تسخين اكل — ده بتاع تذويب الثلج من كتر البرد" |

### Red Lines — What's NOT Funny ❌

- **Politics** — Any political comedy. Especially president, army, security. Not worth the risk.
- **Religion** — No religious jokes. At all. Not even "light" ones.
- **Appearance** — No jokes about someone's looks, weight, height
- **Intelligence** — No "dumb person" jokes. Insulting in Egyptian culture
- **Gender** — No sexist humor. No jokes about women's driving, men's cooking, etc.
- **Tragedy** — No death, accident, illness humor
- **Class** — No mocking poverty or lack of education
- **Generational** — Don't have a brand mock older generations' tech literacy

### Emoji Use with Masri Humor

Egyptians use emojis differently with Masri text:

| Emoji | Masri Meaning | When |
|-------|---------------|------|
| 😂 | Sarcasm, "can you believe this", mild defeat | After relatable fails, ironic statements |
| 👀 | Looking, interested, "tell me more" | Hooks, cliffhangers, "إيه ده؟" |
| 🤝 | Agreement, deal, solidarity | Call to action, offer, team spirit |
| 🔥 | "This is great", fire, approval | Success stories, impressive results |
| 💪 | Determination, strength | Motivational, team work, persistence |
| 💀 | "I'm dead" from laughter/exhaustion | Over-the-top humor, extreme situations |
| 😅 | Awkward but okay | Mistake confessions, "معلش" vibes |
| 🙃 | Ironic, not serious, opposite meaning | Sarcasm in text form |
| 🫡 | Salute, "on it", respect | Committed but suffering (humorous) |

**Rule:** 0-1 emojis per line, max 2 per piece. Over-emoji reads as fake.

### Formal Greeting Hierarchy

Use the right title for the right context:

| Title | Masri | Gender | When |
|-------|-------|--------|------|
| حضرتك | 7adretak / 7adretek | m/f | Default polite "you" — safe with everyone |
| أستاذ / أستاذة | ostaz / ostaza | m/f | Professionals, clients, colleagues (most common in business) |
| باشا | basha | m | Honorable (warm but professional. Not for Gulf clients) |
| مدام | madam | f | Married women in business (common in Egypt) |
| آنسة | anesa | f | Unmarried women (less used now, preferring أستاذة) |
| دكتور | doktor | m/f | PhDs, physicians, university professors |
| مهندس | mohandes | m/f | Engineers (used as title, common in Egypt) |
| معالي | me3aly | m/f | Ministers, high officials (rare in content) |

**Rule of thumb:** When in doubt (email, proposal, meeting), use **حضرتك** or **أستاذ/أستاذة + name**. More respectful than too casual.

### Bilingual Switching in Business Masri

Some English words are so common in Egyptian business Masri that using their Arabic equivalent sounds unnatural:

| Use This (Natural) | Avoid This (Forced) | Context |
|--------------------|--------------------|---------|
| **Deadline** | الموعد النهائي | Projects |
| **Brief** | الموجز / الملخص | Creative work |
| **Proposal** | عرض السعر (ok) / المقترح (too MSA) | Sales |
| **Meeting** | الاجتماع (ok) but "ميتينج" also common | General |
| **Campaign** | الحملة (ok) | Marketing |
| **Target** | الهدف / المستهدف | Goals |
| **Benchmark** | المعيار المرجعي (too MSA) | Performance |
| **ROI** | العائد على الاستثمار (use both) | Results |
| **KPI** | مؤشرات الأداء | Analytics |
| **Feedback** | التغذية الراجعة (too MSA — say "رأيك" or keep feedback) | Reviews |
| **Budget** | الميزانية | Finance |
| **Update** | التحديثات | Progress |
| **Report** | التقرير | Documentation |
| **Strategy** | الاستراتيجية | Planning |
| **Timeline** | الجدول الزمني | Scheduling |

**Rule:** Keep the English term if:
1. It's universally used in Egyptian business speech (deadline, ROI, KPI)
2. The Arabic equivalent is rarely used or sounds academic
3. You're writing for a business audience familiar with these terms
4. But don't mix both languages in the same sentence unless it genuinely flows that way in Egyptian speech
