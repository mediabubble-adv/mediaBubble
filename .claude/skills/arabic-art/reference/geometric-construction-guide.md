# Arabic Art — Geometric Construction Reference

## The 4 Symmetry Families in Islamic Geometric Art

| Family | Number-Fold | Subtypes | Used In |
|--------|-------------|----------|---------|
| Square | 4-fold | 4, 8, 12-pointed stars | Maghreb, Andalusia, North Africa |
| Triangle (Hexagonal) | 6-fold | 6, 12-pointed stars | Persian, Ottoman, Mughal |
| Pentagon (Decagonal) | 5-fold | 5, 10-pointed stars | Mamluk, Seljuk, Fatimid |
| Pentagon (Decagonal) advanced | 10-fold | 10, 20-pointed stars | Late Mamluk, Ottoman Iznik |

## Building a 8-Pointed Star (Khatam — Basic Pattern)

```
Step 1: Draw a circle, divide into 8 equal angles (45° each)
Step 2: Draw two squares offset 45° → one inside the other
Step 3: Extend the intersection points to create the star points
Step 4: Connect outer points of the star → forms an octagon
Step 5: The negative space forms 4 + 4 = 8 smaller shapes
```

## Proportional System — The "Ram" Module

| Term | Meaning | Ratio |
|------|---------|-------|
| Ram | Unit of measurement for the star | 1 |
| Bayt | The "house" between star arms | √2 (1.414) |
| Daman | The overlap between adjacent patterns | 0.5 |

## Materials & Craft Techniques

| Technique | Region | Period | Description |
|-----------|--------|--------|-------------|
| Zillij | Morocco | 12th century+ | Hand-cut glazed terracotta mosaic |
| Qashani | Iran | 14th century+ | Painted ceramic tiles with floral + geometric |
| Kashi | Ottoman | 15th century+ | Iznik tiles; underglaze painting |
| Stone inlay (Ablaq) | Mamluk/Syria | 13th–15th c. | Alternating white/red stone stripes |
| Plaster carving (Girih) | Iran/Central Asia | 10th century+ | Stucco star patterns |
