# Cultural Norms — Core Egyptian Values

## Family Centrality

The family is the fundamental unit of Egyptian society — not the individual.

### Content Implications
- Always frame health decisions as family decisions (not individual)
- Use "أسرتك" (your family) alongside or before "انت" (you)
- Show patients in family context (with parents, spouse, children)
- Avoid messaging that prioritizes individual over family
- Family approval is a key decision factor in healthcare choices

### Masri Phrases That Resonate
| Phrase | Context |
|--------|---------|
| عشان صحتك وصحة أسرتك | Health content — frames individual health as family health |
| فكر في اللي حواليك | Decision-making content |
| احنا في مصر عيلة واحدة | Community / nation-scale content |
| أمك وأبوك يستاهلوا أحسن حاجة | Senior care / elder health |
| عيالك أمانة في رقبتك | Pediatric / family health |

### What to Avoid
- Individualistic framing ("this is YOUR journey" — Egyptian audiences find this cold)
- Showing family conflict or dysfunction in marketing
- Implying independence from family is a positive goal (it's not in Egyptian context)

## Face and Dignity (الوش / الكرامة)

"Face" (الوش) and "dignity" (الكرامة) are paramount in Egyptian culture. Public embarrassment is a major social violation.

### Content Implications
- Never mock, shame, or embarrass the audience or subject
- Never use "before" images that make subjects look degraded
- Frame health issues as common challenges, not personal failures
- Avoid direct criticism of Egypt's healthcare system — frame improvements positively
- Use indirect criticism when needed: "محتاجين نشوف تطور أكتر" vs "النظام فاشل"
- Allow face-saving exits in CTAs — "لما تبقى مستعد" vs "مفيش عذر"

### Masri Phrases That Protect Dignity
| Phrase | Context |
|--------|---------|
| كلنا بنمر بحاجات زي كده | Normalizing health struggles |
| مفيش حاجة تستاهل نهمل صحتنا | Gentle nudge without judgment |
| احنا فخورين بيك إنك بتفكر في صحتك | Affirmation before recommendation |
| مش عيب إن الواحد يسأل | Encouraging help-seeking |

### What to Avoid
- Before/after that implies the person was ugly/unworthy before
- "أنت غلطان" (You are wrong) — use "في طريقة أحسن" instead
- Any suggestion that seeking help is a weakness
- Public call-outs, negative comparisons, or shaming language

## Hospitality (الضيافة / الكرم)

Egyptians pride themselves on hospitality and generosity. This extends to digital spaces.

### Content Implications
- Content should feel welcoming, not transactional
- Free resources, guides, and consultations align with hospitality values
- Use generous language: "احنا هنا عشانك", "تحت أمرك"
- Over-deliver on value before asking for anything
- Thank the audience profusely for their time and trust

### Masri Hospitality Phrases
| Phrase | Context |
|--------|---------|
| أهلين وسهلين بيك في MediaBubble | Welcome on platform |
| تحت أمرك في أي وقت | Ongoing support |
| عيوننا بترمش ليك | Warm hospitality (very Egyptian) |
| الخدمة دي هدية مننا ليك | Free resources / lead magnets |
| شرفتنا بزيارتك | After booking / visit |

### What to Avoid
- Cold, transactional CTAs without warmth
- Asking for payment or commitment before providing value
- Limited-time offers without a generous framing
- Automated / robotic language in personal communication

## Social Hierarchy

Egyptian society respects hierarchy based on age, title, profession, and family background.

### Content Implications
- Always use respectful titles: دكتور, استاذ, حاج, حاجة
- Age = authority in health matters — show older doctors as most authoritative
- Younger professionals should show deference to older patients
- Don't challenge traditional authority structures in marketing
- Government and institutional endorsements carry weight

### Title Usage Guide
| Title | When to Use | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| دكتور | Medical professionals, PhD holders | دكتور أحمد أخصائي القلب |
| أستاذ | University professors, senior professionals | أستاذ محمد مدير المستشفى |
| حاج / حاجة | Older men/women (also, pilgrimage honorific) | حاجة فاطمة، حاج محمود |
| مستر / مدام | Less formal, upper-class context | مدام نادية في الاستقبال |
| باشا / بيه | Very formal/respectful (used less in casual content) | Use sparingly, can feel old-fashioned |

### What to Avoid
- First-name-only address for professionals (unsuitable)
- Portraying young people as authorities over older people in health contexts
- Dismissing traditional or religious health practices as backward
- Implying patients know as much as doctors (respect medical authority)

## Religious Values

Religion (Islam and Christianity) permeates daily life in Egypt.

### Content Implications
- Begin important content with بسم الله or الحمدلله when appropriate
- Reference God positively: إن شاء الله, الحمد لله, ربنا يشفيك
- Frame health outcomes within God's will — "ربنا يتمم لك على خير"
- Don't be afraid of religious references in marketing (Egyptians expect them)
- Respect both Muslim and Christian audiences (use inclusive religious framing)

### Religious Phrases for Content
| Phrase | Context |
|--------|---------|
| ربنا يديك الصحة والعافية | Health blessing (neutral) |
| إن شاء الله في ميزان حسناتك | Thanking someone for health effort |
| الحمد لله على سلامتك | Post-recovery greeting |
| ربنا يشفيك ويعافيك | Get-well wish |
| توكلنا على الله | Before a procedure / decision |

### What to Avoid
- Atheistic or secular-only framing (alienates majority)
- Mocking or questioning religious practices
- Using religious phrases flippantly or in commercial-only contexts
- Ignoring Christian Egyptian audience (make content inclusive)
- Referring to alcohol, pork, or haram activities positively
