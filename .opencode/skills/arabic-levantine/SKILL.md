# arabic-levantine — Levantine Arabic (Shami)

## Overview

Levantine Arabic (اللهجة الشامية) is the dialect continuum spoken in the Levant region: Syria, Lebanon, Palestine, and Jordan. It is the second most widely understood Arabic dialect group after Egyptian, due to Levantine media, diaspora communities, and regional trade.

## National Distinctions

### Syrian Arabic

| Feature | Damascene | Aleppine | Notes |
|---------|-----------|----------|-------|
| Qaf | Hamza / 2 | 2 (same) | But q → 2 is a marker of urbanity |
| Long ā | ā | ē (imala) | Aleppine imala: شام → Shēm |
| Second-person fem. pl. | -kun | -kon | |
| Vocabulary | هون (here), قدّام (in front) | نفس (here) | Distinct sets |

### Lebanese Arabic

| Feature | Beiruti | Northern | Notes |
|---------|---------|----------|-------|
| Qaf | Hamza / 2 | Qurʾānic ق or glottal | Tripoli area retains qaf |
| Emphatic shift | Heavy 6/9 patterns | Less emphatic | Beiruti more distinctive |
| French loanwords | Heavy (merci, bonjour, ascenseur) | Moderate | Code-switching to French is a social marker |
| Interrogative | شو (shu) | إيش (aysh) | |

### Palestinian Arabic

| Feature | Urban (Madani) | Rural (Fallahi) | Bedouin |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|---------|
| Qaf | Hamza / 2 | Kaf / k | Gaf / g |
| Kaf | k | ch / tsh (in some areas) | k |
| Future | raḥ- | ḥa- | b- |
| Vowels | More open | More closed (imala) | Conservative |

### Jordanian Arabic

| Feature | Urban (ʿAmmān) | Rural | Bedouin |
|---------|----------------|-------|---------|
| Qaf | Hamza / 2 | Gaf / g (north) / k (south) | Gaf / g |
| Kaf | k | k | ch (k → č) |
| Vocabulary | Levantine core + Gulf influence | Traditional Palestinian | Bedouin (Khaliji-leaning) |

## Core Grammar

### Present Tense

Prefix **b-** (indicative/habitual) and **ʿam- + b-** or **ʿam- + verbal noun** (progressive):

| Person | Indicative | Progressive |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| I | بعمل (baʿmil) | عم بعمل (ʿam baʿmil) |
| You (m) | بتعمل (btaʿmil) | عم بتعمل (ʿam btaʿmil) |
| You (f) | بتعملي (btaʿmili) | عم بتعملي (ʿam btaʿmili) |
| He | بعمل (byaʿmil) | عم يعمل (ʿam yʿammil) |
| She | بتعمل (btaʿmil) | عم بتعمل (ʿam tʿammil) |
| We | منعمل (mnaʿmil) | عم نعمل (ʿam nʿammil) |
| They | بعملوا (byaʿmilu) | عم يعملوا (ʿam yʿammilu) |

### Future Tense

Prefix **raḥ-** or **ḥa-**:

- raḥ- is dominant in Syrian, Lebanese, Palestinian urban
- ḥa- is more common in rural Palestinian and Jordanian
- Example: رح روح / حا روح (raḥ rūḥ / ḥa rūḥ — I will go)

### Negation

- Present/future: **mā** + verb + **-sh** (ماعملهاش)
- Non-verbal: **mish** or **mū** (مش / مو)
- Past: **mā** + past verb + **-sh** (ما عملتش)
- Imperative: with **lā** + **-sh** suffix (لاتعملش)

## Cultural Context

### Formality Register

| Level | Name | When |
|-------|------|------|
| 1 | Deep colloquial | Peer chat, comedy |
| 2 | Daily Shami | Social media, casual content |
| 3 | Polite Shami | Brand content, customer-facing |
| 4 | Formal Shami | Articles, professional content |
| 5 | MSA | Legal, official, academic |

### Politeness Conventions

- Title + first name: أستاذ / حضرتك / ست (for women)
- Refusal ritual: refuse twice politely before accepting
- Hospitality: offer must be declined 2–3 times before accepting food/drink
- Family questions: asked as politeness, not intrusion (How's the family? etc.)

### Taboo Topics

Same as Egyptian: politics, religion, sex, profanity. Additional Levantine specifics:
- Sectarian identity (Sunni/Shia/Christian/Druze) — never assume or label
- The Syrian war, the Palestinian cause, the Lebanese civil war — tread with extreme care
- Israel — avoid direct naming in Palestinian context unless content is explicitly political
- French mandate legacy in Lebanon, Turkish mandate legacy in Syria — historically sensitive
