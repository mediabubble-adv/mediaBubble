# Tafsir — Classical Works Annotated Bibliography

## Primary Classical Tafsirs (مصادر التفسير بالمأثور)

| Work | Author | Died | Method | Unique Value |
|------|--------|------|--------|--------------|
| جامع البيان عن تأويل آي القرآن | Ibn Jarir al-Tabari | 310 AH / 923 CE | Narration chain + opinion; length ~30 vols | The gold standard; contains asbab al-nuzul (reasons of revelation) |
| تفسير القرآن العظيم | Ibn Kathir | 774 AH / 1373 CE | Narration + simplification | Readable, widely distributed; balances detail and analysis |
| بحر العلوم | Abu al-Layth al-Samarqandi | 373 AH / 983 CE | Hanafi tafsir | Unique perspective; legal rulings derived from Hanafi school |
| معالم التنزيل | al-Baghawi | 516 AH / 1122 CE | Narration + Sunni creed | Strong on aqidah implications; polemical against interpretation extremes |
| الدر المنثور في التفسير بالمأثور | al-Suyuti | 911 AH / 1505 CE | Pure narration | No opinion — only hadith and athar; essential for asbab al-nuzul research |

## Classical Tafsirs with Ratio (عقلي / اجتهادي)

| Work | Author | Died | Method | Unique Value |
|------|--------|------|--------|--------------|
| مفاتيح الغيب (التفسير الكبير) | Fakhr al-Din al-Razi | 606 AH / 1210 CE | Philosophical; ~35 vols | Deepest rational analysis; addresses every conceivable objection |
| الكشاف عن حقائق التنزيل | al-Zamakhshari | 538 AH / 1144 CE | Linguistic + Mu'tazilite | Unmatched linguistic insight; used by all later mufassirin but with caution on creed |
| أنوار التنزيل وأسرار التأويل | al-Baydawi | 685 AH / 1286 CE | Concise Zamakhshari without Mu'tazila | Standard madrasa text; balances language, law, and creed |
| إرشاد العقل السليم إلى مزايا الكتاب الكريم | Abu al-Su'ud | 982 AH / 1574 CE | Ottoman Hanafi | Insightful Ottoman-era commentary; strong on i'rab and rhetoric |
| روح المعاني | al-Alusi | 1270 AH / 1854 CE | Late Ottoman; encyclopedic | Synthesizes all prior tafsir traditions; ~30 volumes |

| Work | Author | Born/Died | Notable For |
|------|--------|-----------|-------------|
