# Seasonal & Cultural Marketing Hooks

Organized by season/event. Each entry includes: timing, Masri hook example, content angle, and sensitivity notes.

## Ramadan (Ramadan — Variable, ~March/April)

**Timing:** Full month (29–30 days). Campaigns start 2 weeks before.

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Pre-Ramadan urgency | "رمضان قرب — جهز حملاتك قبل الزحمة" |
| Ramadan opener | "رمضان كريم! النهارده هنقولكم إزاي تزود مبيعاتك في الشهر الكريم" |
| Mid-Ramadan engagement | "نص رمضان عدى — لسه فيه وقت تظبط حملاتك" |
| Pre-Eid | "العيد قرب — آخر فرصة عشان تجهز حملاتك قبل العيد" |

**Content angles:**
- Family, generosity, community themes
- Evening content (iftar time onward) gets highest engagement
- Reduced working hours — campaigns should run in evening/night
- Food and beverage content after maghrib only

**Sensitivity:** No food/drink imagery during fasting hours. No "Ramadan diet" or weight loss content (tone-deaf). Focus on spiritual and family values.

## Eid al-Fitr & Eid al-Adha

**Timing:** 3–4 days each. Prepare 1–2 weeks ahead.

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Eid greeting | "كل سنة وانت طيب — عيد مبارك" |
| Pre-Eid push | "جهز نفسك للعيد — عروضنا لسه شغالة" |
| Post-Eid recovery | "العيد خلص — نرجع للشغل بقوة؟ 💪" |

**Content angles:**
- Eid clothing, gifts, outings for B2C
- Pre-Eid urgency for service businesses ("جهز حملاتك قبل العيد")
- Post-Eid: "New month, new goals" for B2B

**Sensitivity:** Eid morning (first day) content is inappropriate — everyone is at prayer/with family. Afternoon onward is fine.

## Back to School (August–September)

**Timing:** Mid-August to early October. Peak: first 2 weeks of September.

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Urgency | "قرب الدراسة — حان وقت تجهيز حملات المدارس والسناتر" |
| Value | "المدارس فتحت — والطلاب محتاجين شنط، كتب، وزي رسمي" |
| Digital | "أولياء الأمور دلوقتي على الإنترنت — استهدفهم بالإعلانات الصح" |

**Content angles:**
- Target: educational services, retail (school supplies), tutoring centers
- Also: parents seeking kids' activities, tech products for students
- Facebook/Instagram ads targeting parents 30–50

## Sham el-Nessim (Spring Festival — April)

**Timing:** Day after Coptic Easter (Monday). Niche but culturally significant.

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Greeting | "ربيع نضيف عليكم — كل سنة وانتم طيبين" |
| Seasonal | "الربيع بدأ — واستعد لموسم شم النسيم" |

**Content angles:**
- Outdoor activities, parks, restaurants, delivery services
- Niche opportunity for food, beverage, retail
- Not a major spending event — low-budget campaigns

**Sensitivity:** Keep light, positive. No religious references even though it coincides with Coptic Easter.

## Summer & Red Sea Season (May–September)

**Timing:** May (start) → September (taper). Peak: June–August.

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Pre-summer | "الصيف في الغردقة — استعد للموسم" |
| Peak | "الなんج الصيفية بدأت — احجز مكانك قبل الزحمة" |
| Last call | "آخر شهر في الصيف — متضيعش الفرصة" |

**Content angles:**
- Tourism: hotels, diving, water sports, transfers
- Service businesses serving tourists (restaurants, shops, taxi services)
- "Slow season prep" for Sept–Oct (smart businesses plan ahead)

**Geographic specificity:** Hurghada, Sharm el-Sheikh, Marsa Alam, Dahab, Alexandria, North Coast.

## White Friday / Black Friday (Late November)

**Timing:** Friday after US Thanksgiving. Egypt follows "White Friday" convention.

| Hook Type | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Pre-event | "الجمعة البيضا جاية — جهز عروضك دلوقتي" |
| Day-of | "الجمعة البيضا — خصومات مش هتتكرر 💥" |
| Last hours | "آخر ساعات في الجمعة البيضا — استغل الفرصة" |
| Post-event | "الجمعة البيضا خلصت — بس عروضنا لسه شغالة لفترة محدودة" |

**Content angles:**
- B2C: major discounts, limited-time offers
- B2B: annual plans at discount, "lock in 2024 pricing"
- High competition — differentiation is key
- Email + social + ads = full funnel

**Sensitivity:** Don't use "Black Friday" — "الجمعة البيضا" is the local term. "Black" has negative connotations in Arabic-speaking markets.

## National Holidays

| Date | Holiday | Content Angle |
|------|---------|---------------|
| Jan 7 | Coptic Christmas | Greeting only. No promotions. |
| Jan 25 | Revolution Day | Sensitive. Avoid commercial content entirely. |
| Apr 25 | Sinai Liberation Day | Positive, patriotic. Low-key. |
| May 1 | Labor Day | Target employees and businesses. |
| Jun 30 | June 30 Revolution | Sensitive. Avoid. |
| Jul 23 | Revolution Day | Positive, patriotic. Mild promotional ok. |
| Oct 6 | Armed Forces Day | Very sensitive. Avoid promotional content. |

**General rule for national holidays:** If the holiday honors the military/police/president, do not publish promotional content. Post a respectful greeting at most. If the holiday is cultural/labor/spring, mild promotional content is acceptable.

## Crisis & Downtime

| Scenario | Content Strategy |
|----------|-----------------|
| National tragedy | Pause all promotional content for 24–72 hours. Post solidarity message if appropriate. |
| Economic crisis | Avoid luxury/upsell content. Focus on value, ROI, necessity. |
| Security incident | Pause location-targeted ads in affected areas. |
| Weather emergency | Pause tourism content in affected regions. |
