# arabic-secular-observances — National & Secular Observances

## Overview

Each of the 22 Arab League member states has a distinct set of national holidays, secular observances, and cultural festivals. Combined with Islamic and Christian religious observances, these form the comprehensive "Agazat al-A'yad" (عجازات الأعياد / holiday periods) calendar for the Arab world.

## "Agazat al-A'yad" — Comprehensive Holiday Framework

For each Arab League context, the full Agazat Calendar integrates:

| Category | Calendar Type | Source of Dates |
|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Islamic holidays | Lunar (shifting ~11 days/year) | Ministry of Awqaf + moon sighting |
| Christian holidays | Fixed + Julian/Gregorian | Church authorities |
| National holidays | Gregorian fixed | Law/public announcements |
| Cultural festivals | Announced yearly | Ministry of Culture/tourism |
| School holidays | Academic year (Sep–Jun) | Ministry of Education |
| Bank holidays | Align with major Islamic + national | Central Bank |

### Sectoral Variation in Holiday Observance

| Sector | Typical Observance |
|--------|-------------------|
| Government | Follows official calendar strictly; all public holidays observed |
| Private sector | Some flexibility; major holidays observed; smaller holidays may pass |
| Banking | Very strict (follows Central Bank announcements); usually full Islamic + national holidays |
| Schools | Separate academic calendar with summer (Jun–Aug), spring/winter breaks |
| Media/Content | Peak activity before/during all major observances; reduced staffing on public holidays |
