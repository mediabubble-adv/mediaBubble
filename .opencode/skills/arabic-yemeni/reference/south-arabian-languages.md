# Yemeni — South Arabian Languages Primer

## The Modern South Arabian Languages (MSALs)

These are not Arabic dialects — they are **separate Semitic languages** predating Arabic in the region.

| Language | Speakers | Region | Status |
|----------|----------|--------|--------|
| Mehri (مهرية) | ~170,000 | Eastern Yemen (Mahrah), Oman (Dhofar), Saudi border | Vital; used for daily life |
| Soqotri (سقطرية) | ~70,000 | Socotra Island (Yemen) | Endangered due to diaspora |
| Shehri / Jibbali (شحرية) | ~70,000 | Oman (Dhofar mountains) | Vital |
| Bathari (بثرية) | ~200 | Oman coast | Critically endangered |
| Harsusi (حرصوصية) | ~3,000 | Oman interior | Endangered |
| Hobyot (هبيوت) | ~100 | Yemen/Oman border | Critically endangered |

## Mehri — Most Spoken MSAL

| Feature | Mehri | Arabic |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Consonants | Preserves ancient Semitic ض lateral fricative | Lost in Arabic |
| Gender | Three genders: masculine, feminine, common | Two: masculine, feminine |
| Plural | Broken + sound + external (more complex than Arabic) | Broken + sound |
| Vocabulary | Arabic loanwords from recent contact | Arabic itself |

## Why This Matters for Content

- A Yemeni speaker from Mahrah likely speaks **Mehri as L1**, Arabic as L2 — with a strong Mehri accent in Arabic
- Soqotri speakers may have limited Arabic fluency
- Marketing Arabic content to these groups: use **standard Yemeni Arabic** with awareness that Arabic is NOT their mother tongue
- Do NOT assume that "Arabic" content reaches all Yemenis — South Arabian language regions need different approaches

## Key Cultural Distinctions

| Aspect | South Arabian Speakers | Arabic Speakers |
|--------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Identity | Tribal/regional identity first, Arab identity second | Arab identity first |
| Poetry | Rich MSAL poetic tradition (not Arabic meter) | Arabic khalīl meter |
| Music | Non-Arabic modal systems | Arabic maqam |
