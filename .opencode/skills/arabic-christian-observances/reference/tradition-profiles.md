# Christian Observances — Arab Christian Tradition Profiles

## Major Traditions in the Arab World

| Tradition | Population (Arab World) | Countries | Language of Liturgy | Distinctive Feast |
|-----------|------------------------|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Coptic Orthodox | ~10–15 million | Egypt, Sudan, Libya | Coptic + Arabic | Nayrouz (Coptic New Year), Christmas (7 Jan) |
| Greek Orthodox (Rum) | ~3–5 million | Syria, Lebanon, Jordan, Palestine | Greek + Arabic | Easter (Julian date) |
| Maronite Catholic | ~3 million | Lebanon, Syria | Syriac + Arabic | Mar Maroun (9 Feb) |
| Syriac Orthodox | ~1 million | Syria, Iraq, Turkey | Syriac + Arabic | Easter (Julian) |
| Syriac Catholic | ~250,000 | Syria, Iraq, Lebanon | Syriac + Arabic | — |
| Chaldean Catholic | ~500,000 | Iraq, Syria | Syriac + Arabic | Christmas (25 Dec) |
| Armenian Apostolic | ~400,000 | Lebanon, Syria, Jordan | Armenian + Arabic | Easter (Gregorian + Julian mix) |
| Latin Catholic | ~500,000 | All countries | Latin + Arabic | Christmas (25 Dec) |
| Coptic Evangelical | ~500,000 | Egypt | Arabic | Christmas (7 Jan) |
| Anglican/Episcopal | ~200,000 | Egypt, Sudan | Arabic + English | — |

## Calendar Systems

| Tradition | Christmas | Easter (Pascha) System |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------|
| Coptic Orthodox | 7 January (Coptic calendar) | Julian (usually April–May) |
| Greek Orthodox (Old Calendar) | 7 January | Julian |
| Greek Orthodox (Revised) | 25 December | Gregorian (~50% of parishes) |
| Maronite | 25 December | Gregorian |
| Syriac Orthodox | 25 December (some: 6 Jan Epiphany) | Julian |
| Chaldean | 25 December | — |
| Armenian Apostolic | 6 January (Theophany combined) | Gregorian |
| Roman Catholic | 25 December | Gregorian |

## Sensitivity Notes for Content

| Topic | Approach |
|-------|----------|
| Muslim–Christian relations | Emphasize shared cultural heritage; avoid theological comparison |
| Cross/crucifix imagery | Use with context; crosses are identity symbols, not decorative |
| Phrases like "Christ died for us" | Accurate for Christian audiences but considerate in mixed contexts |
| References to Israel | Sensitive for Arab Christian audiences (Palestinian Christians' Al-Aqsa affinity) |
