# arabic-islamic-observances — Islamic Holidays & Observances

## Overview

Knowledge of Islamic holidays and observances across the Arab world is essential for content scheduling, business operations, marketing campaign timing, and understanding cultural rhythms. This skill covers the Hijri (Islamic lunar) calendar, all major and minor observances, country-specific holiday durations, and the cultural meanings and practices associated with each.

## The Hijri Calendar

The Islamic calendar is lunar: 12 months totaling ~354 days, approximately 10–12 days shorter than the Gregorian year. All Islamic holidays shift ~10–12 days earlier each Gregorian year.

## Major Observances

### Ramadan (شهر رمضان)

The most significant month in the Arab (and Muslim) world. Content, business rhythms, media consumption, and social life transform completely.

**Key dates:**
- Moon sighting (Istihlal) triggers start — official announcement varies by country
- Laylat al-Qadr (27th night believed most likely) — Night of Power
- Last 10 days (al-Ashar al-Awakher) — I'tikaf (spiritual retreat in mosque)

**Cultural profile by sub-phase:**

| Phase | Days | Behavior | Content Opportunity |
|-------|------|----------|-------------------|
| First 10 | 1–10 | Adjustment; high energy; shopping | Ramadan prep, food/recipes |
| Middle 10 | 11–20 | Fatigue; deeper spiritual focus | Reflection, tips for mid-Ramadan |
| Last 10 | 21–30 | Peak spirituality; Laylat al-Qadr | Laylat al-Qadr, donation, spirituality |
| Before Eid | Last 2–3 | Shopping for Eid (clothes, gifts, food) | Eid prep content, last-minute |

**Agazat al-A'yad — Ramadan:** Not a holiday; it is a month of modified working hours. Government and private sector: start time delayed by 1–2 hours, end time earlier by 1–2 hours. Full fasting month adjustments.

### Hajj & Eid al-Adha (عيد الأضحى)

**Key dates timing:**
- 8 Dhul-Hijjah (Yawm al-Tarwiyah): Hajj begins
- 9 Dhul-Hijjah (Yawm 'Arafah): Day of Arafah — fasting recommended for non-pilgrims
- 10–13 Dhul-Hijjah: Days of Eid al-Adha + Tashriq

**Agazat duration (generally shorter than Eid al-Fitr for most countries):**

| Country | Government | Private |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Saudi Arabia | 7–10 days (Hajj holiday, not just Eid) | 4–7 days |
| UAE | 5–7 days | 3–5 days |
| Qatar | 5–7 days | 3–5 days |
| Most others | 2–4 days | 2–3 days |

**Eid al-Adha traditions:** Udhiyah (sacrifice of sheep/goat/cow), meat distribution (1/3 family, 1/3 relatives/neighbors, 1/3 poor), Takbirat (special glorifications of God), more solemn than Eid al-Fitr.

### Ashura (10 Muharram)

**Sunning significance:** Day of fasting (recommended: 9th + 10th). Commemorates:
- Moses' rescue from Pharaoh (fasting of thanks)
- Passes over: also the day Karbala occurred (Shia mourning — highly significant)

**Shia observance:** Intense mourning ('Azadari), processions, matam (chest-beating). Iraq (Karbala, Najaf), Lebanon (Nabatiyeh, Beirut southern suburbs), Bahrain, eastern Saudi Arabia (Qatif, al-Ahsa), Syria (Sayyidah Zaynab). This is an **extremely sensitive** observance: do not conflate or mix Shi'a and Sunni traditions.

## Moon-Sighting Protocol

- The Islamic months **begin with verified sighting** of the crescent moon (hilal)
- Moon-sighting committees exist in every country — Saudi Arabia's Supreme Court announcement is often (but not always) followed by others
- **Different countries sometimes start holidays on different days** — this is normal and accepted
- For business planning: use Saudi/UAE announcements for Gulf, Egypt/Azhar for Egypt and parts of Levant, local announcements for others
- Common disagreement: first day of Ramadan and of Eid al-Fitr can differ by 1 day between countries
