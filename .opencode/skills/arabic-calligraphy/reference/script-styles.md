# Arabic Script Styles — Classical & Modern

## Classical Six Pens (Al-Aqlam Al-Sittah)

The canonical six scripts that form the foundation of Arabic calligraphy.

### 1. Kufic (Kufi) — الكوفي

**Origin:** Kufa, Iraq — 7th century
**Personality:** Geometric, angular, architectural
**Key traits:**
- Straight horizontal strokes, sharp angles
- Minimal or no diacritical marks (early Kufi)
- Extended horizontal baselines
- Square/rectangular letter proportions

**Modern Sub-styles:**
- **Geometric Kufi** — Interlocking geometric letterforms, popular for logos
- **Square Kufi** (Murabba') — Letters fit into square grids, architectural
- **Floral Kufi** — Decorated with foliate/plant motifs
- **Woven Kufi** — Interlacing letterforms
- **Eastern Kufi** (Mashriqi) — More curved, decorative terminals

**Best for:** Logos, headlines, architecture, tech brands, short text
**Not for:** Body text, long-form content

### 2. Naskh — النسخ

**Origin:** Refined by Ibn Muqlah (10th century), standardized by Ibn al-Bawwab
**Personality:** Elegant, readable, classic
**Key traits:**
- Curved, flowing strokes with precise proportions
- Clear distinction between ascending and descending letters
- Moderate, balanced letter sizes
- Well-defined baseline rhythm

**Modern usage:** Default text face for most Arabic print and digital publishing. The basis for most Arabic typefaces (including Cairo).

**Best for:** Body text, editorial, publishing, long-form content
**Not for:** Short display pieces needing drama

### 3. Thuluth — الثلث

**Origin:** Developed in the 9th century, perfected by Mustafa Rakin
**Personality:** Majestic, dramatic, monumental
**Key traits:**
- One-third of each letter slopes (hence "Thuluth" meaning "one-third")
- Tall ascending strokes with sweeping curves
- Dense, layered compositions
- Wide spacing, generous proportions
- Ornamental potential — often used with decorative elements

**Modern usage:** Titles, ceremonial documents, mosque inscriptions, luxury branding
**Best for:** Headlines, logos, certificates, architectural inscription
**Not for:** Small sizes, body text, digital interfaces

### 4. Diwani — الديواني

**Origin:** Ottoman court script — 16th century
**Personality:** Flowing, ornate, regal
**Key traits:**
- Extreme curvature, letters flow into each other
- Sharp bends and tight loops
- Ascending letters often bend backward
- Dense, compact composition
- Highly decorative — almost illegible to untrained readers

**Sub-style:** **Diwani Jali** — Even more ornate, with stacked letterforms and extensive decoration

**Best for:** Formal documents, luxury brands, perfume, certificates, art
**Not for:** Legibility-critical applications, digital UI

### 5. Ruq'ah — الرقعة

**Origin:** Ottoman Empire — 18th/19th century
**Personality:** Casual, brisk, everyday
**Key traits:**
- Short, straight strokes
- Minimal curvature
- Small letter size relative to line height
- Simplified letterforms
- Quick, efficient pen movement

**Modern usage:** The everyday handwriting script across the Arab world. Digital Ruq'ah is common in casual branding.

**Best for:** Casual brands, food menus, retail, handwritten-style logos
**Not for:** Formal brands, luxury, small digital text

### 6. Maghribi — المغربي

**Origin:** Islamic Spain (Al-Andalus) and North Africa
**Personality:** Rounded, open, distinctive
**Key traits:**
- Wide, rounded curves below the baseline
- Deep, sweeping descenders
- Open counters (letter interiors are spacious)
- Distinctive "wavy" baseline
- Unique letter shapes for Fa', Qaf, and other letters

**Sub-styles:** Sudani, Tunisi, Andalusi, Fassi

**Best for:** North African brands, cultural/heritage identities, editorial
**Not for:** Pan-Arab audiences outside North Africa

## Classical Decorative Styles

### Tughra — الطغراء
Monogram-like calligraphic emblem used by Ottoman sultans. Today used for luxury logos, government seals. Features stacked letterforms, curved strokes, and decorative elements.

### Musalsal — المسلسل
"Chained" script where letters are connected in an unbroken chain. Used for borders, decorative bands, continuous inscriptions.

### Muraba' — المربع
Square/geometric compositions — each letter is squared into a grid. Popular in modern architecture and tech logos.

## Modern & Contemporary Scripts

### Free-Style / Hurufiyya
20th century art movement treating Arabic letters as abstract visual forms. Not bound by classical rules. Used in fine art, experimental logos, fashion.

### Contemporary Kufi
Kufi's geometric DNA makes it the most adaptable classical script for modern design. Contemporary Kufi:
- Breaks classical proportions freely
- Incorporates negative space
- Combines with Latin letterforms
- Uses color and texture

### Calligraffiti
Arabic calligraphy meets graffiti/street art. Popular with younger audiences. Features:
- Bold, thick strokes
- Spray can textures
- Urban color schemes
- Mixed with Latin graffiti styles

### Generative / Computational
Algorithmic Arabic calligraphy using parametric design. Letters generated by code. Used for:
- Dynamic logos
- Motion graphics
- Data visualization
- Interactive installations

## Script Selection by Brand Personality

| Brand Vibe | Primary Script | Secondary Option | Avoid |
|------------|---------------|------------------|-------|
| Luxury heritage | Thuluth | Diwani | Ruq'ah |
| Modern tech | Geometric Kufi | Contemporary | Thuluth |
| Casual/friendly | Ruq'ah | Naskh | Diwani |
| Cultural/artistic | Diwani Jali | Free-style | Square Kufi |
| Editorial/publishing | Naskh | Thuluth titles | Ruq'ah |
| Architecture/real estate | Square Kufi | Geometric Kufi | Diwani |
| Fashion/creative | Contemporary | Free-style | Classical Naskh |
| Food/retail | Ruq'ah | Modern Kufi | Thuluth |
| Financial/corporate | Geometric Kufi | Naskh | Diwani Jali |
| Sports/energy | Calligraffiti | Contemporary Kufi | Thuluth |

## Historical Timeline

```
7th C ─── Kufi (earliest Quranic script)
         └── Eastern Kufi ──→ Maghribi (Andalus)
10th C ── Naskh standardized by Ibn Muqlah
         └── Thuluth established
16th C ── Diwani (Ottoman court)
18th C ── Ruq'ah (Ottoman everyday)
20th C ── Hurufiyya / Free-style
21st C ── Contemporary Kufi / Calligraffiti / Generative
```

## Key Calligraphers (Legacy)

| Calligrapher | Era | Known For |
|--------------|-----|-----------|
| Ibn Muqlah | 10th C | Codified the classical six scripts, proportional script system |
| Ibn al-Bawwab | 10th-11th C | Perfected Naskh, wrote the first decorated Quran |
| Yaqut al-Musta'simi | 13th C | Refined the six scripts, massive influence on Ottoman calligraphy |
| Hamid al-Amidi | 19th-20th C | Revived Kufi, influential on modern Arabic typography |
| Mustafa Rakin | 19th C | Ottoman master of Thuluth |
| Hashim al-Baghdadi | 20th C | Modern Iraqi master, influential across scripts |
| Mohamed Zakariya | Contemporary | American master, reviving Ottoman traditions |

## Regional Preferences

| Region | Preferred Scripts | Cultural Notes |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| Egypt | Naskh (main), Thuluth (display), Ruq'ah (casual) | Strong Thuluth tradition for religious and ceremonial |
| Levant | Naskh, Thuluth | Similar to Egypt, slightly more Ruq'ah in casual use |
| Gulf | Kufi (modern), Contemporary | More open to experimental/contemporary approaches |
| North Africa | Maghribi, Naskh | Maghribi is a strong regional identifier |
| Iraq | Eastern Kufi, Naskh | Deep Kufi heritage |
| Turkey | Thuluth, Diwani (Jali) | Strong Ottoman calligraphy tradition |

## Script Anatomy Quick Reference

| Feature | Kufi | Naskh | Thuluth | Diwani | Ruq'ah | Maghribi |
|---------|------|-------|---------|--------|--------|----------|
| Baseline | Straight | Curved | Dipping | Wavy | Straight | Deep wavy |
| Ascenders | Short/medium | Tall | Very tall | Tall, bent | Short | Short |
| Descenders | None | Moderate | Deep | Deep, curled | Shallow | Very deep, wide |
| Stroke weight | Uniform | Moderate variation | Heavy variation | Extreme variation | Light variation | Moderate, rounded terminals |
| Letter spacing | Wide | Moderate | Wide | Compact | Tight | Wide |
| Diacritics | Minimal | Full | Full, decorative | Full, integrated | Minimal | Full |
| Proportion | Square | 1:2 (height:width) | 1:3 | Variable | Compact | 1:2 with deep descenders |
