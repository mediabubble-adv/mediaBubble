# arabic-khaliji — Gulf Arabic (Khaliji)

## Overview

Gulf Arabic (اللهجة الخليجية) spans the Arabian Peninsula from the Hijaz to Oman. It encompasses Saudi Arabia's four major regional dialects (Najdi, Hejazi, Eastern Gulf, Southern), the national dialects of the Gulf Cooperation Council states (UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Bahrain), and Omani Arabic. Historically shaped by Bedouin heritage, pearl-diving and seafaring culture (for coastal areas), and oil-era demographic transformation.

## National & Regional Distinctions

### Arabic Saudi Sub-Dialects

| Feature | Najdi (Central) | Hejazi (Western) | Gulf Eastern | Southern (Asir/Jizan) |
|---------|-----------------|------------------|--------------|----------------------|
| Qaf | Gaf / g | Hamza / 2 (urban) / g (Bedouin) | Gaf / g | Gaf / g |
| Kaf | k | k | k → č in some contexts | k |
| Jīm | j | j (MSA-like) | y in some words | j |
| Future | raḥ- or ḥa- | raḥ- | raḥ- / b- | sa- (MSA-like) |

### Emirati Arabic

| Feature | Emirati | Notes |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Jīm → Yā | ج → ي | جلس → يلس (yilas, to sit) |
| Kāf → Čīm | ك → چ | كيف → چيف (chēf, how) |
| Qaf | Gaf / g | قال → گال (gāl) |
| Vocabulary | Persian/Indian heavily | يمه (yammah, mother), هال (hāl, cardamom), بوم (būm, owl) |

### Qatari & Kuwaiti

| Feature | Qatari | Kuwaiti |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Qaf | g | g |
| Kaf → Ch | Yes (limited) | Yes (widespread) |
| Sharqawi (seafaring) vocabulary | Moderate | Strong |

### Bahraini

Bahrain has a socially stratified dialect continuum:
| Group | Dialect Features |
|-------|-----------------|
| Sunni (urban) | Closer to other Gulf dialects, q → g |
| Shia (Baharna) | Preserves q → q, more Aramaic substrate, distinctive vocabulary |
| Rural | Bedouin-leaning, conservative |

### Omani Arabic

| Feature | Northern (Muscat) | Interior (Dakhiliyya) | Dhofar (Southern) |
|---------|-------------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Qaf | g | g | g (with South Arabian substrate) |
| Kaf | k | k → ch in some | k |
| Vocabulary | Marked by Swahili, Portuguese, English (seafaring) | Conservative Bedouin | South Arabian (Mehri) influence |

## Core Grammar

### Present & Future

- Future with **raḥ-** (common across GCC): راح أسوي (raḥ asawwī — I will do)
- Future with **b-** (Kuwaiti/Qatari preference): بسوي (basawwī)
- Present with **y-...-ūn** plural for masculine: يلعبون (yalʿabūn)

### Negation

- Verb negation: **mā** + verb + **-sh** (limited use in Gulf compared to Egyptian/Levantine)
- Non-verbal: **mub** / **mū** (مو / موب) — distinctive Gulf feature
- Prohibition: **lā** + verb (no circumfix in classical Gulf)

### Possession

- Attached pronouns: -i / -ya (my), -ak / -ich (your m/f), -ah / -ha (his/her), -na (our), -hum (their)
- Independent: مالي / مالك / ماله etc. (māl + suffix) — equivalent to بتاع in Egyptian

## Cultural Context

### Hospitality & Social Code

- Greeting sequence: Salām → Kaif ḥālak → Ahlan wa sahlan → gahwa (coffee) cycle
- Coffee ritual: 3 servings minimum offer, slight shake of cup signals done, never fill your own cup
- First-name basis only with explicit permission; use family/clan title otherwise
- Majlis culture: elders enter first, speak first, sit in the honored position
- Conservative dress code expectations in public-facing content

### Business Etiquette

- Wasta (واسطة) — relationship-based networking is essential; cold contact is unproductive
- Meetings: flexible start times, extensive small talk before business
- Gender: observe stricter gender boundaries in professional contexts than in Levant/Egypt
- Hierarchy: decisions defer to the most senior person present

### Taboo Topics

- Ruling families (Āl Saʿūd, Āl Nahyān, Āl Thānī, Āl Ṣabāḥ, Āl Khalīfa, Āl Saʿīd) — never criticize
- Oil economy — sensitive to external critique
- Tribal genealogy — sensitive and complex; do not oversimplify or misstate
- Religious authority — GCC states derive legitimacy from Islam; avoid any suggestion of secular critique
- LGBTQ+ — criminalized in all GCC states; do not reference
- Alcohol — prohibited in Saudi, Kuwait; regulated in UAE, Qatar; acknowledge sensitivity
