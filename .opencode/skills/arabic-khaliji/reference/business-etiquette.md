# Gulf Arabic — Business & Social Etiquette

## The Majlis (مجلس) — Meeting Protocol

| Position | Etiquette |
|----------|-----------|
| Seating | Host sits furthest from door; guest of honor sits to host's right |
| Entering | Greet oldest/most senior first (even if you don't know them) |
| Welcoming | "أهلاً وسهلاً" — reply "أهلاً بك" / "الله يسلمك" |
| Coffee (قهوة) | Hold cup in right hand; do not set it down until host gestures to pour again |
| Empty cup | Wiggle cup = "please pour" (convention); empty cup handed back = "I'm done" |
| Leaving | Stand only after the host stands; do not rush out |

## Gahwa (Arabic Coffee) Protocol

| Action | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Accept with right hand | Respect |
| Accept with both hands | High respect / gratitude |
| Sip 1–3 times then pass back | "Thank you, I'm full" |
| Refuse (press right hand to heart) | "I'm fasting" / health reason |
| Host refills from same pot | Continuous hospitality |
| Pass thermos instead of pot | Less formal / busy setting |

## Wasta (واسطة) — The Social Capital System

In Gulf business culture, **wasta** (personal connection/intermediary) is essential:

| Domain | Wasta Needed | Without Wasta |
|--------|--------------|--------------|
| Job application at government | Required; >50% faster | 6–18 months delay |
| Business license | Recommended | Delays / rejections |
| Meeting with senior official | Essential | Will not happen |
| Hospital access | Helps for top specialists | Standard queue |

⚠️ Wasta is not bribery — it's the **legitimate** social capital and reputation system. The person providing wasta vouches for you.

## Gender Sensitivity in Business

| Situation | Best Practice |
|-----------|---------------|
| Meeting a Gulf businesswoman | Let her extend hand first; do not initiate handshake |
| Speaking to mixed group | Address the senior person (not specifically by gender) |
| Emails | Use "السيد/السيدة" (Mr./Ms.) until told otherwise |
| Gift giving | For female colleagues: flowers/chocolate = acceptable but give through male colleague in conservative settings |
| Dress code | Men: suit or thawb+ghutra; women: modest abaya or conservative international business wear |
