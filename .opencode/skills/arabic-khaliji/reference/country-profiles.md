# Gulf Arabic — Country Linguistic Profiles

## Saudi Arabia

| Sub-region | Dialect | Key Feature | Qaf | Future | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------------|-----|--------|-------|
| Najd | Najdi | Purest Bedouin | g | raḥ- / ḥa- | Conservative; tribal lexicon |
| Hejaz (urban) | Hejazi | Cosmpolitan | 2 (hamza) | raḥ- | Mix of influences; pilgrimage trade |
| Hejaz (Bedouin) | Hejazi B. | Rural marker | g | b- | Merging into Najdi |
| Eastern Province | Gulf Eastern | Shared with KSA East | g | raḥ- | Arabic + Farsi influences (Hasa) |
| Asir/Jizan | Southern Yemeni-leaning | Yemeni overlap | g | sa‑ / raḥ- | Close to Yemeni |

## UAE

| Emirate | Feature | Jīm | Kāf | Vocabulary |
|---------|---------|-----|-----|------------|
| Abu Dhabi | Standard Gulf | j → y (ylis for jalas) | k → ch (chēf) | Bedouin heritage; oil administration |
| Dubai | Cosmopolitan Gulf | j → y (varying) | k → ch (variable) | Mix of nationalities; English/Tagalog influence |
| Sharjah | Conservative | j → y | k → ch | More traditional; cultural capital |
| Fujairah | Omani-leaning | j (preserved) | k (preserved) | Closer to Omani; Indian Ocean vocabulary |

## Qatar & Kuwait

| Feature | Qatari | Kuwaiti |
|---------|--------|---------|
| Qaf → g | Strong | Strong |
| Kaf → č | Present but weakening | Very strong (علامچ) |
| Persian vocabulary | Moderate | Moderate (pearl-diving heritage) |
| Sharqawi (sea) lexis | Significant | Very significant (دشه, بوم, لنش) |
| Bedouin markers | More conservative | Mixed urban/Bedouin |

## Bahrain

| Community | Dialect | Distinction |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Sunni (urban ruling) | Bahraini Khaliji | q → g, k → č, close to Qatari |
| Shia (Baharna) | Bahraini Baharna | q preserved, k preserved, Aramaic substrate, unique vocabulary |
| Rural | Bedouin-derived | q → g / j → y, more conservative |

## Oman

| Region | Dialect | Features |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Muscat/Seeb | Omani standard | q → g, k → k, Swahili/Portuguese loanwords |
| Interior (Nizwa) | Interior Omani | Conservative; q → g (strong), tribal lexicon |
| Dhofar (Salalah) | Dhofari | South Arabian (Mehri) substrate; Arabic with Mehri phonology |
| Musandam | Musandami | Closer to Emirati; isolated mountain dialect |
