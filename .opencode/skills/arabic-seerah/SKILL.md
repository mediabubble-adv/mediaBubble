# arabic-seerah — Prophetic Biography (Seerah)

## Overview

Seerah (السيرة النبوية) is the biography of the Prophet Muhammad ibn Abdullah (570–632 CE / 53 BH–11 AH). It is the foundational narrative of Islam, establishing the context in which the Quran was revealed and the model (uswah hasanah) for Muslim life. Seerah study is divided into the Meccan period (13 years of da'wah under persecution) and the Medinan period (10 years of state-building).

## Chronological Framework

### Pre-Revelation (570–610 CE / 53 BH–13 BH)

| Year | Event | Significance |
|------|-------|-------------|
| ~570 CE | Year of the Elephant (عام الفيل) | Abraha's attack on Mecca; Prophet's birth year |
| 576 CE | Death of mother Amina | Orphaned at 6 |
| 578 CE | Death of grandfather Abd al-Muttalib | Under uncle Abu Talib's care |
| 583 CE | Journey to Syria; meeting with Bahira | Christian monk recognition of prophethood signs |
| ~595 CE | Marriage to Khadijah | First wife; 25 years; first to convert |
| ~605 CE | Black Stone placement (Hajar al-Aswad) | Prophet's arbitration prevented tribal war |

### Meccan Period (610–622 CE)

| Phase | Years | Key Events |
|-------|-------|------------|
| Secret da'wah | 610–613 | First revelation (Iqra'), first converts (Khadijah, Abu Bakr, Ali, Zayd), al-Arqam house |
| Open da'wah | 613–615 | "And warn your nearest kinsmen" (26:214); persecution begins |
| Abyssinian migration | 615 | First Hijra to Christian King Negus (al-Najashi) — 15 Muslims |
| Social boycott | 616–619 | Banu Hashim in Shi'b Abi Talib; Khadijah and Abu Talib die (Year of Sorrow / عام الحزن) |
| Ta'if | 619 | Failed da'wah journey; humiliation and stoning |
| Isra' & Mi'raj | 620 | Night Journey to Jerusalem and ascension to heavens |
| Second Aqaba pledge | 622 | 75 Medinese pledge to protect the Prophet |

### Medinan Period (622–632 CE)

| Year AH | Key Events |
|---------|------------|
| 1 (622) | Hijra, Constitution of Medina, mosques built |
| 2 (624) | Qibla change (al-Aqsa → Ka'bah), Battle of Badr, Zakat obligation, Ramadan fasting |
| 3 (625) | Battle of Uhud, prohibition of alcohol |
| 4 (626) | Banu Nadir expulsion, Tayammum revealed |
| 5 (627) | Battle of the Trench (Khandaq), Banu Qurayza |
| 6 (628) | Treaty of Hudaybiyyah, Khaybar, letters to world leaders |
| 7 (629) | Conquest of Khaybar, Umrah al-Qada |
| 8 (630) | Conquest of Mecca, Battle of Hunayn, Tabuk |
| 9 (631) | Year of Deputations (Sanat al-Wufud), Surah Al-Imran revealed |
| 10 (632) | Farewell Pilgrimage (Hajjat al-Wada'), Ghadir Khumm |
| 11 (632) | Death of the Prophet Muhammad (12 Rabi' al-Awwal) |

## Key Thematic Areas

### The Constitution of Medina

The first written constitution in Islamic history, establishing:
- A multi-religious polity (Muslims, Jews, polytheists bound by treaty)
- Mutual defense obligations
- Religious freedom for Jews and Christians
- A legal framework for conflict resolution

### Military Campaigns (Ghazawat & Saraya)

| Battle | Date | Significance |
|--------|------|-------------|
| Badr | 2 AH | Decisive victory; 313 vs 1000; established Muslim military credibility |
| Uhud | 3 AH | Muslim defeat due to archer disobedience; lesson in obedience |
| Khandaq | 5 AH | Defensive trench strategy (Salman al-Farisi's idea); coalition of tribes defeated |
| Khaybar | 7 AH | Conquest of Jewish fortress settlement |
| Mu'tah | 8 AH | First campaign against Byzantines; Zayd/Ja'far/Abdullah ibn Rawaha martyred |
| Conquest of Mecca | 8 AH | Bloodless; general amnesty (أمن عام) — model for victory conduct |
| Tabuk | 9 AH | Largest army (30,000); no battle; diplomatic deterrent |

### Major Treaties

| Treaty | Terms | Significance |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Hudaybiyyah (6 AH) | 10-year truce, return of refugees, free tribal affiliation | Seen as surrender by some Companions but called "clear victory" (48:1) |
| Constitution of Medina | See above | First Islamic pluralistic governance |

## Primary Sources

| Source | Author | Date | Strength |
|--------|--------|------|----------|
| Sirat Rasul Allah | Ibn Ishaq (d. 150 AH) | Lost original; preserved in Ibn Hisham's recension | Earliest full biography |
| Al-Sirat al-Nabawiyya | Ibn Hisham (d. 218 AH) | Most widely used classical source | |
| Al-Tabaqat al-Kubra | Ibn Sa'd (d. 230 AH) | Biographical dictionary of Companions | Companion-level detail |
| Tarikh al-Rusul wa al-Muluk | Al-Tabari (d. 310 AH) | Universal history including Seerah | Strong chain documentation |
| Al-Bidaya wa al-Nihaya | Ibn Kathir (d. 774 AH) | Blend of Seerah and hadith | Critical approach |

## Lessons & Contemporary Application

### Governance & Leadership
- The Shura (consultation) model — decisions made through community deliberation
- The Qudwa (role model) principle — a leader must model what they ask of others
- Mercy in victory (Conquest of Mecca: 10,000 army enters with amnesty)
- Treaty ethics (Hudaybiyyah: terms accepted despite appearing unfavorable)

### Social & Interfaith
- The Constitution of Medina's protection of religious minorities
- Respectful engagement with Christians (Najran delegation received in mosque)
- Justice for non-Muslims under Islamic governance

### Military Ethics
- Prohibition of killing non-combatants (women, children, elderly, monks)
- Prohibition of mutilating bodies (Muthlah)
- Prohibition of treachery and treaty-breaking
- Proportionality in response
