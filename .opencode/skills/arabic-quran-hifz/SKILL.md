# arabic-quran-hifz — Quran Memorization Proficiency

## Overview

Hifz al-Quran (حفظ القرآن) is the memorization of the entire Holy Quran (6,236 verses across 114 Surahs and 30 Juz'). A Hafiz (حافظ) is someone who has committed the full Quran to memory. This skill covers the science and methodology of Hifz, including curriculum design, muraja'ah (revision) systems, error detection, and long-term retention.

## Memorization Stages

### Stage 1: Foundation (Foundation Quran Reading Fluency)

**Prerequisites** before beginning Hifz:
- Fluent reading of the Quran in Arabic with correct Makharij
- Basic Tajwid rules (Noon Sakinah, Meem Sakinah, Qalqalah, basic Madd)
- Familiarity with Juz' 'Amma (last section, Surahs 78–114) as starting point

### Stage 2: Incremental Memorization (التحفيظ التدريجي)

| Component | Description | Benchmark |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| New lesson (sabaq / سبق) | Daily new material | 3–7 lines (10–20 ayahs) |
| Recent revision (sabqi / سبقي) | Past 7 days of memorization | Full 7-day cycle daily |
| Full revision (manzil / منزل) | All previously memorized material | 1/5–1/3 of total daily |

**Rate benchmarks:**
- Full-time Hifz (specialized schools): 1–2 pages/day → complete in 2–3 years
- Part-time (evening/weekend): 0.5–1 page/day → complete in 4–6 years
- Intensive (summer programs): up to 3 pages/day → supervised approach only

### Stage 3: Error Detection & Recitation Testing

Measurement criteria for assessment:

| Metric | Measurement |
|--------|-------------|
| Accuracy rate | % of verses recited without error in a single pass |
| Fluency rate | Verses per minute at tarteel pace (slow, measured) |
| Tajwid compliance | % of Makharij, Madd, and Ghunnah rules correctly applied |
| Stamina (continuous) | Maximum verses recited from memory without break |

## Muraja'ah (Revision) Protocol

### Daily Muraja'ah Structure

1. **Pre-sleep recitation**: Recite the previous day's sabaq + sabqi during night prayer (Tahajjud)
2. **Morning confirmation**: Recite to teacher/instructor — graded on accuracy
3. **Written review**: Write the sabaq passage from memory; compare to Mushaf
4. **Listening check**: Recite to a partner who follows in the Mushaf; partner marks all errors

### Weekly Muraja'ah Cycle

| Day | Activity |
|-----|----------|
| Days 1–5 | New sabaq + rolling sabqi |
| Day 6 | Full-week manzil revision |
| Day 7 | Cumulative test: 5 random passages from all previously memorized |

### Long-term Muraja'ah (After Hifz Completion)

| Frequency | Coverage | Duration |
|-----------|----------|----------|
| Daily | 1/5 Quran (4–6 pages) | 1 hour |
| Weekly | 1/5 Quran (6 Juz') | 2 hours |
| Monthly | Full Quran (khatm) | 5–6 hours over 2–3 days |
| Annual | Intensive khatm with tajwid audit | 1–2 weeks |

## Mutashabihat (Similar Verses)

A distinct sub-skill: verses that are partially or fully identical across different Surahs.

| Technique | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Pattern recognition | Identify verse pairs/groups with identical first half, different second half |
| Keyword association | Link Surah name + distinctive keyword to the correct verse |
| Mnemonic chain | Build a cross-referential mental chain for each similar pair |
| Juz' mapping | Know which Juz' contains each member of a similar pair |

**Example:** The repetition of the verse "فَبِأَيِّ آلَاءِ رَبِّكُمَا تُكَذِّبَانِ" occurs 31 times in Surah Ar-Rahman (55). Each occurrence follows a different blessing. A Hafiz must know which blessing comes before each repetition.

## Assessment & Certification

### Grading Rubric

| Grade | Accuracy | Fluency | Tajwid | Definition |
|-------|----------|---------|--------|------------|
| A+ | 99–100% | 10+/min | 95%+ | Mastery — minimal prompting needed; errors self-corrected |
| A | 95–98% | 8–9/min | 85–94% | Strong — occasional non-self-corrected errors |
| B | 85–94% | 6–7/min | 75–84% | Developing — multiple errors per page |
| C | 70–84% | 4–5/min | 60–74% | Emerging — frequent errors; significant loss |
| D | <70% | <3/min | <60% | Needs restart from previous checkpoint |

### Hifz Ijazah

The completion of Hifz is marked by an ijazah (license) from the teacher, often in a formal khatm ceremony. The ijazah:
- Certifies that the student has memorized the entire Quran
- Verifies that the student can recite with correct tajwid
- Traces the teacher's chain of transmission back to the Prophet
- Authorizes the student to teach and transmit the Quran to others
