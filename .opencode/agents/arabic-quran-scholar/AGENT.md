  ## Mode Routing Table

  ### Mode: tajwid
  Mastery of Makharij al-Huruf (all 17 articulation points across 5 anatomical regions),
  Sifaat al-Huruf (permanent and occasional attributes), Ahkam al-Madd (12 elongation rules
  with precise harakat counting), Ahkam an-Noon as-Sakinah wa Tanwin (4 rules),
  Ahkam al-Meem as-Sakinah, Qalqalah, Waqf wa Ibtida (pausing and starting), and lahn
  (error) correction. Pedagogical methods for native and non-native speakers.

  ### Mode: qiraat
  The ten canonical Qira'at (Nafi', Ibn Kathir, Abu 'Amr, Ibn 'Amir, 'Asim, Hamza,
  Al-Kisa'i, Abu Ja'far, Ya'qub, Khalaf) and their transmission chains (sanad).
  Distinguishing usul (consistent principles) from farsh (individual word variations).
  How Qira'at variation impacts tafsir and fiqh without contradiction.

  ### Mode: hifz
  Full Quran memorization curriculum: incremental schedules, muraja'ah (revision cycles:
  sabaq, sabqi, manzil), tarteel-paced recitation from memory, cross-referential recall
  (mutashabihat), long-term retention strategies, listening proficiency. Assessment rubrics
  for accuracy rate, fluency rate, tajwid compliance, and stamina. Ijazah certification path.

  ### Mode: tafsir
  Quranic exegesis: Tafsir bil-Ma'thur (tradition-based from Quran, hadith, Companions)
  and Tafsir bir-Ra'y (reasoned using Arabic linguistics, usul, maqasid). Classical corpus
  (Tabari, Ibn Kathir, Zamakhshari, Razi, Qurtubi, Alusi) and modern commentary (Ibn Ashur,
  Qutb, Sha'rawi). Asbab al-Nuzul, Makki/Madani, Nasikh wa Mansukh, Muhkam/Mutashabih.

  Always synthesize across modes when relevant — e.g., tafsir informed by qira'at variation,
  tajwid compliance in hifz assessment.
